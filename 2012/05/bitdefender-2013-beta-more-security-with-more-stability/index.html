Bitdefender 2013 Beta : More Security With More Stability!

May 26th, 2012 | Comments

According to many independent test lab results, Bitdefender is the number one Anti Virus in the market. It have a very good detection rate and it can keep you safe from most of the malware threats out there. Recently, they started to work on their next step… Bitdefender 2013. I kept testing it for several days and here’s my review…

Last year, when Bitdefender released the version 2012, I was really exited and installed it on my system right away. It have a good detection rate, but it had a lot of other bugs and stability issues. Bitdefender is the Anti Virus which crashed most in my tests. Kaspersky and Norton were really solid. But I took the risk because Bitdefender had a really good record on catching the pests. But, it slowed down my browsing and downloading severely. I contacted them and they also tried their best to solve it. But, after waiting for several days, I quit. It also had a bad habit of taking too many resources. But that’s the past…

Thinking they might have solved the issues, I tried the new Bitdefender 2013. First I tested the download and browsing speeds. They are really good and didn’t feel any slowdowns. The resource usage is also lower than before and it loads really fast. They seems to have fixed the big issues!

The new Bitdefender have a slightly new UI. No glossy looks. Now it have a mat finish. With the essential features, as usual, Bitdefender offers a lot more features to protect our PC.

If you have a notebook computer, you can use their new anti-theft feature to protect it against normal thieves who don’t have any knowledge to hack and steal your data. If your device is lost, you can now locate it, wipe it completely or even lock it down completely.

Earlier, Bitdefender started to test on a new tool called the USB Immunizer. These days, Flash drives carry most of the Viruses around the world. With Bitdefender’s new USB Immunizer, you can make your USB immunized for such malware. Bitdefender 2013 have this tool built right into it.

Now here’s something cool. Bitdefender’s new desktop widget… Its something really useful. You can launch the app through it. It also shows the current activity of the Firewall and the Antivirus with cool progress bars. If you have any notifications, they’ll also pop up in this widget.

I know this is a really good antivirus. But what I wanted to test is its stability and the resource usage. I should say that it worked really well in my machine. Stable, and because of their Autopilot feature, the program also remained silent making all the decisions by itself! After they release the final release, I might consider giving Bitdefender another try. What do you think?

Here’s a video review of Bitdefender 2013 made by Languy99. According to it, BD 2013 managed to catch many malware threats than others. (Better than Norton 2013). In here, it crashed a lot. I think it’s because of the test machine is a virtual machine. I never faced that problem in my real machine!
