Updated : Facebook to Buy Opera and Conquer the Browser Market?

May 25th, 2012 | Comments

I just heard some disturbing news… According to the latest rumors (from Pocket-lint.com), Facebook is trying to buy Opera! Opera is a rising start in the browser world. Its one of the most feature rich browsers in the world. The number one mobile browser in the world. With the upcoming Opera 12, I’m hoping to see a really stable browser which can directly hit Chrome. But, there’s a big problem…

Some people might think that Facebook buying Opera is a good thing because Facebook have money and they’ll try their best to get to the top of the browser world. But, I don’t think I’ll like the idea…

If Google buy another company, it’s a good sign because Google will boost that company and most probably, make that product the number one in the world. If Facebook buy a company, most of the time, they close the company and take the staff. They then introduce their own versions which we don’t even care much.

Facebook and Google both have bad records on privacy. I think Facebook is the worst case. They don’t think about the user. They want money. Google might be bad but making a large portion of their browser open source show that they are not that bad. They want to work with the community. But I don’t think Facebook is like that.

If Facebook buy Opera, sometimes, they’ll close down the company and introduce their own browser which will be full of Facebook stuff. Look at Google… The browser is really simple and that’s why we love it. Opera is also getting close to simplicity. They try to balance the power of features and simplicity. But now, everything can be ruined.

I thought of taking a better look at the upcoming Opera 12 because it seem to be really stable. But if Facebook buy it, I’ll think again… What do you think? Do you want Facebook to buy Opera? Do you want it to stay solo?
