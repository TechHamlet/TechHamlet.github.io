How to Make Infographics Go Viral?

Dec 17th, 2012 | Comments

When people hear about the usage of infographics, they associate it with internet phenomena that go viral. This is not true for all forms of infographics; but there are simple measures that can be taken to improve the chances of any form of infographic going viral and thus bring lots of exposure to the subject matter and highlight it. The main reasons infographics play a major role in online marketing and internet traffic control is that they provide a break from any long written article and are known to catch the reader’s attention and eye balls more easily than lines and lines of text. They also allow us to quickly pass on pertinent information to people who are more interested in a summarized version of an article and its topic and in vastly improving any website’s usability and content quality.

The most important factor to consider when one is dealing with infographics for the public that is meant to go viral is targeted traffic. Targeted traffic allows more people the chance to read and know about infographics via the usage of professional press releases. When these public announcements are made on a few well chosen directories, they are automatically subjected to a steady stream of new people who have their own network of contacts they can spread the world of the infographic to.

Most viral infographics posts began as blog posts which were written by specialized search engine optimization writers with a specific target audience in mind. The more hits that the blog post gets, the more times the infographics are read, since almost everyone has their attention grabbed by the more eye-catching informative graphics rather than lines of text. The readers of the blog would share the post on social networking sites and this would add another layer of traffic that spreads exponentially with each instance of reposting or sharing, thereby vastly increasing exposure.

There are a number of specific online directories that make a business out of infographic promotion, and most of the posts or articles that end up here are those that have already permeated the social media and are in the process of obtaining exposure among professional audience involved in the infographics industry.

The next step to increase visibility of infographics is to attract business contacts that one already has into promoting or broadcasting the subject matter in their contact networks. This form of link-building strategies promotes linkages between different sites like guest blogging about the said infographic material or just simply providing URLs back to the site in question from other pages on the web.

The proper usage of these internet traffic building strategies creates large levels of awareness for posts and can help infographics go viral. Another less used, but extremely effective method of making a post or infographic go viral is utilisation of internet meme communities, which allows users to post pictures and videos onto their website. Other users can then view the post and either vote that they like the content or dislike it. It can be seen that the more the likes that a particular post has, the higher its position in the voting page.

Once a post has more than a certain number of votes, it is marked as trending. The concept of ‘trending topics’, meaning posts that are in the beginning stages of the process of going viral, has stemmed from social networking media giant Twitter. With the increased usage of hashtags, it is now possible to monitor the appearance and usage of particular phrases in the internet. The final stage of a post going viral is that it is classified as a ‘hot topic’. These communities form a very unique group of internet users who can make a post go viral in a matter of hours. From creation to global recognition in a day is a pretty good usage of infographic data manipulation.

Though it takes some effort to create a post that can be called informative, entertaining and eye catching, it is that trifecta of properties that constitute a post that truly has the makings of infograph that can go viral. If you can achieve this tall order, then you have the ability to make an internet sensation out of your subject material.
