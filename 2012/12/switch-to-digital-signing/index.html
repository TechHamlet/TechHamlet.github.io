A Big Believer of the 'Go Green' Concept? Switch to Digital Signing!

Dec 12th, 2012 | Comments

Image Source : Aunt Owwee

As traditional industries and businesses worldwide have recognized the increasing environmental consciousness and hence have ‘gone green’. Whether it’s chemical industries or car industries, the trend paradigm has shifted gears to a more ‘Go Green’ work mantra. Going paperless is one of those ‘save the environment’ campaigning strategy that has corporate sector attains increased efficiency and hiked up the business processes.

Meeting the challenge to reduce paper dependence may seem tough, but it has been simplified with the help of digital technologies. By automating the processes by involving digital techniques that not only are secure and also legal, they get the tasks done and generate optimal results 10x times more quickly than the traditional methods.

Since, in this competitive era, businesses cannot afford to stay behind – one missed step and they might fall back in the stampede. With the advent of digital signatures, it has redeemed businesses to an advanced level of growth and prosperity. Digital signatures are resourceful, not to mention, affordable and have immensely helped businesses to go green by replacing the paper and ink based tasks with digital and secure methods.

With the de-forestation, growing at an alarming rate, it ultimately becomes responsibility of every organization to cut down on their paper use, to save the trees. These digital signatures offer a means to help contribute to this noble endeavor. By going paperless, businesses not only assist in donating to this cause, but they also continue to serve their own ‘commercial’ purposes as well. Every country around the world has implemented laws in order to make digital signatures and hence, making these digitally signed documents valid and legal. This way, digital signatures have enabled governments and executive sectors to advance to paperless business handling, which has reduced the response time and at the same time, keeps the work transparency intact.

By going paperless, businesses can proficiently save money, space, and upsurge the work productivity with the automated processes for both the internal as well as external communications. Online transactions or communications depend strictly on secure and authentic means for data protection. For that matter, it is essential that the information requires legal and valid mechanism that guarantees data security and non-repudiation, by all means. And that is exactly what digital signatures do – they help businesses ascend up higher on the ladder of ‘Go Green’ objectives using paper-free methods.

In today’s times, meeting that exorbitant expenditure, while maintaining the business productivity is a challenge on its own. Digital signatures have molded and cut-down the expense practice of businesses by introducing paperless methods to undertake important tasks. This allows businesses to save time and money, as well as manpower.

Take a look at procurement methods for example. The traditional procurement process involves bulky paperwork and almost never-ending manual work efforts. E-Procurements helpful for both buyers and suppliers since it condense the processing time and reduce the unnecessary paper work. For sustaining the security and authenticity of the processes, businesses have indulged in becoming more digital-savvy by replying on digital signatures. That’s a win-win situation for both consumers and businesses.

‘Going green’ has helped businesses in supplementing their customer support initiatives in a more quick and cost-efficient manner. Apart from governmental sectors, public and private sectors such as banking, telecom, real-estate, insurance companies and even health care departments have adopted online platforms for improving their services and efficacy.

From digitally signing bank statements or sending/receiving financial reports or even taking care of online loan applications, with adoption of digital signatures in the e-banking sector, it has helped customers and banking institutions to save on their business investment time by going paperless. Encrypted reports can easily be delivered to customers in bulk and in no time. For decrypting the document, customers are assigned with a key to preserve the privacy and of course, data integrity. Since it disregards the necessity for any physical presence, it speeds up the entire process and wins customers over and strengthens their belief in the service.

Digital signatures are in fact helping everyone endorse the eco-friendly technology and have helped business to continue expanding in a smarter, secure and authentic manner.

A vast majority of companies have already moved to digital signing and are having millions annually; so what about you?
