How to Reduce Your Home Phone Bill and Maximise Your Home Broadband

Dec 8th, 2012 | Comments

The current economic situation has led to us all looking for areas where we can look to cut costs. Reducing a monthly phone bill is one area where money can be saved, these easy to follow tips will give you some ideas as to how you can reduce the cost of your monthly phone bills, and how you could save by switching your phone package too.

Change the way you pay

 Paying by Direct Debit is the cheapest way to pay your phone bill, and often gets you a reduction on your line rental. If you don’t already pay by Direct Debit, it’s well worth getting in touch with your current supplier to find out if it would save you money.

Change your call package

Today, phone companies provide a range of call packages, designed to suit different types of phone user. Take a look at your last bill to get a better idea of your typical monthly usage. Ask yourself the following when you look at your bill details:

Do you make most of your calls during the day, or at evenings and weekends?

Are you a heavy or a light phone user?

Do you call a lot of mobile numbers?

Are most of your calls local or national – and do you make any international calls?

 It could be that your current call package isn’t the best one for your individual call needs, and there may be a cheaper, more suitable package out there, either with your current provider or with another.

Let your supplier know you’re thinking of switching

 et in touch with your current supplier and ask them about your current usage. They’ll be able to tell you if you’re on the best available package. When you let them know you’re shopping around for deals elsewhere, they won’t want to lose your custom, and they’ll often do their best to offer you a deal, or switch you to a better package.

Shop around and save

Online comparison tools make it easier than ever to find out if you’re paying too much for your calls, and they make it simple to switch too. Once you’ve contacted your provider to find out their best deal, use this information to compare their deal with other providers. It only takes a few minutes, and the whole transfer procedure can be done online now, so you might not even have to make any calls in order to switch.

Consider a bundled package

If you don’t already receive your calls and line rental, or calls, line rental and broadband from the same company, you could be overpaying. A bundled package from one supplier is typically the cheapest way to get connected.

These packages are also far easier to manage, and to budget for, streamlining the billing process. Internet service providers regularly offer discounts on these bundled packages, including Sky, whose current offer is 6 months free unlimited home broadband when you switch your broadband, calls and line rental to them, whether you’re a new or existing customer.
