The Ongoing Computerization of the Car

Feb 4th, 2012 | Comments

With the introduction of the assembly line to the production of automobiles, Henry Ford was able to make cars affordable to the average American household. The same process was then introduced to the production of computers, also making them affordable for most people. Today, it is common to see both of these revolutionary technologies integrated, with computer consoles increasingly being built into the dashboards of cars.

Here are a few ways that computers and network connectivity are being integrated to cars to make them more like rolling living rooms:

Your type of music

With a computer console and a connection to the internet through wireless networks, you can have your choice of Pandora, Sirius radio, iTunes or Google music playlists, or just classic radio. Gone are the days of listening to 6 minutes of commercials for the gamble of hearing a song you like afterwards.

Mobile Theaters

Many vans, SUVs, and cars are starting to come with built-in monitors on the console and in the back of seats. You can control and choose your favourite movie or TV show for road trips and vacations. An added bonus to this feature is the silencing of the dreaded question: ‘are we there yet?’

Calendar Integration

As one of the more useful features with the computerization of cars, you can now stay connected to everything that you have to do throughout the day in an easy, wreck-free fashion. Instead of rushing in the mornings or trying to check your phone while you drive, your car has the ability to update you with important information for your day on the commute to work.

Navigation with personalized suggestions

Every technological advance always seems to upgrade to the ‘smart’ version after a few years; smart cars, smart phones, and now smart navigation. It is one thing to be able to find directions to any place that you know you want to go to, but what happens when you need suggestions? The integration of social media and local businesses into services like Google maps and Mapquest has made it is easier than ever to search for a category of place rather than a specific brand, and services like Google latitude can easily direct you to wherever your friends happen to be.

Self-driving capabilities

Cars truly are being created with minds of their own. Many cars come equipped with cameras, motion sensors, and even depth perception to be able to provide safer parking and avoid crashes. While this is more of a safety feature than a fun one, it is still a great advancement in the computerization of cars.

Technology might not be at the level of the Jetsons yet, but there are some pretty amazing developments going on in the world of computers and transportation. As technology continues to grow at an exponential rate, it will be very interesting to see what the future holds as more and more technologies are integrated for a seamless communication and travel.
