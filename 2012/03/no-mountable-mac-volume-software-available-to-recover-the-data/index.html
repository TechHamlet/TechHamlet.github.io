No Mountable Mac Volume Software Available to Recover the Data?

Mar 6th, 2012 | Comments

Sometimes, after booting up your Mac you may find that some volumes do not get mounted automatically. Even when you try to mount them manually, they failed to mount. The reason behind this problem is the corruption of those volumes, which has barred them from being mounted and all the data in them has become completely inaccessible thereof. However, you can still be able to recover data from the no mountable volumes with the help of any advanced Mac data recovery software.

Daily you deal with many different kinds of files, among which some contain important business critical or official information, whereas some others contain memorable photos, videos and other similar files. If they all will be stored in a single volume of the system, the entire system will just mess up. This is the reason, for easy storage and access of required files; separate volumes are made on the hard drive. Another not so visible benefit of having separate volumes for different categories of data is that if by any means one volume gets corrupt, you can still have access to the other volumes.

But, if your Mac has a single volume or the one volume, storing really important data has become corrupt, there are ample reasons to worry. There are a number of reasons, which are responsible for the corruption or damage to the volumes and make them unmountable. Below listed are some of the prominent reasons in this regard:

  • Infection to the volume by malicious program, virus or malware

  • Deletion of necessary system files or operating system crash

  • Damage due to improper system shut down

  • Severe corruption to the file system

  • Contradiction with newly installed hardware or software application

For the above situations, Apple suggests running one repairing volume repairing utility, ‘Disk Utility’. Disk Utility is the inbuilt utility, with which you can verify the integrity of Mac volumes and repair them, if needed. But, there are times, when the volumes have been severely damaged and even Disk Utility fails to repair them.

In such situations, you need to take the help of any third party Mac recovery software to perform data recovery from no mountable volumes. There are many such reliable software in the market. These software are developed with powerful algorithms to recover lost or inaccessible data from any volume that has been inaccessible, irrespective of the reason. Moreover, the software come with interactive graphical user interfaces and are immensely easy to use.
