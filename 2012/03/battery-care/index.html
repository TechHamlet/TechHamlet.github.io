Battery Care : Keep Your Notebook Battery in Top Shape!

Mar 9th, 2012 | Comments

One of the most important parts in your laptop is the battery. If you mess with it, you can lose your laptop and even the main use of a laptop… mobility! Before buying my own lap, I have dealt with several laps from my friends. Some brought them to fix them. All of them have screwed up with their battery. Those machines cannot be switched on without the battery. Have to plug them in. No point of having a laptop which you can use without power right? That’s why we need to be careful when using a notebook.

There are some rules to follow when you are using a notebook to protect the battery. There are things you can do to reduce the load and use the battery for a longer period. But, some people don’t know about them. Some don’t have time to care about it. But, there’s a solution…

Recently, I stumbled on a cool app named Battery Care which does exactly what the name say. I installed it a week ago and started to test it. The app is very light on resources and won’t bother you after you set it up.

When you are in battery power, Battery Care can automatically switch your power plan to a power saving plan and disable some heavy system services like Windows Aero. This can really boost the battery usage.

After discharging and charging the battery for several times, the battery meter will start to give an inaccurate reading. The machine will power off while the meter is showing that you have some more time to live. To fix this, you need to calibrate the battery… A full discharge followed by a full charge. Normally, its hard to track this and calibrate the battery when needed. But, Battery Care can do the tracking. With the default settings, it will ask you to re calibrate the battery after 30 discharge cycles. You can also set it to notify you when the battery level is too low.

It also helps us to monitor the CPU and disk temperatures to make sure that things are not getting too hot. Too much heat can also degrade the health of your battery. According to Battery Care, you should remove the battery if you are plugged in and if you are going over 60 Celsius. Otherwise, its safe to keep the battery in the lap fully charged and power plugged in.

Now, if you really care about your battery, you should take care of it manually, or install Battery Care. I also recommend you to check their Guide to learn more about your notebook battery. I learnt a lot from it. It answered several questions I had in my mind about charging!
