Checklist for Maintaining Your Computer

Jan 31st, 2012 | Comments

A stitch in time saves nine, you must have surely heard of this saying. Since we are using the computer so often in our everyday life and we download a lot of items from the internet therefore our computer gets burdened with a lot of data within a very short period of time. Subsequently it is but natural for it to assume a no-response status.

Now, you might not be aware of this, but each time you use your computer to read mails, create files or open files from the internet, you are actually adding to the storage space of your computer.There are certain operating systems which are inherently equipped to keep the machine free from spam and ensure its continued functioning in a proper manner. This means that in the event of your computer slowing down and performing in a sluggish rate, you would know that it is time for maintenance.

However, instead of shelling out a large amount for its maintenance, adhering to a checklist to see whether everything is in order or not would not just save time but money as well. Some basic checks for maintaining the computer’s hardware are -

  • Whether the CPU, monitor, computer and printer are properly plugged into a surge protector is a point which should be checked upon foremost.

  • Use alcohol-free wipes or a gentle cleaning solution for the purpose of cleaning various parts of the computer such as keyboard, mouse, screen, etc.

  • The connections of various devices should permit unhindered air flow and these needs to be ensured to facilitate problem-free working of the device.

  • If there is any accumulated dust in or around your computer then get rid of it. Your motherboard is most vulnerable to accumulation of dust and debris and therefore you need to clean the area from time to time.

  • Cooling fan of your CPU is one of the most important components contributing towards the proper functioning of the computer and hence keeping it free from cobwebs and dust is one of the measures is a chore which would enable it to function properly.

Likewise there is a checklist for maintaining the software as well which entails the following practices -

  • You should have updated the virus configuration and anti-virus program at all times. Conducting a full system virus scan every week is also strongly recommended.

  • If you haven’t got rid of the temporary internet files in your computer then you should get into the habit of doing so. In case you are using Internet Explorer then you simply need to click on the Tools Menu and then on Internet options. Once there you can delete whatever options you want to.

  • The internet cache should also be cleared often because the computer stores web pages and pictures of the websites you visit and over a period of time these clog the amount of free space in your hard drive.

  • Clearing your recycle bin every once in a while will ensure that your hard drive remains free of clutter and hence functioning optimally.

  • A disk cleanup is also recommended so that the old compressed files and temporary files can be deleted in addition to the junk. All you need to do is to go to Start Menu followed by programs, accessories, system tools and finally disk cleanup and your objective is accomplished.

  • While it may be more convenient for you to have shortcut icons on your desktop, you should get rid of the ones that are not really necessary to make your desktop look neat and also to save space.

  • For enhancing the efficiency of your machine gets rid of all those programs which are hardly used on a regular basis. You can go to the control panel and then click on the ‘Add or Remove programs’ option. Once there, you will be able to see a list of products on your computer which have been installed as also the frequency of usage of such programs.

  • Keeping a backup your entire database in case of unprecedented failure of the system is another noteworthy option.

  • Also, operating the disk defragmenter to create space on your hard driver which has been used up for the storage of unnecessary data is an effective option. Just go to start button, then click on programs, accessories and then system tools after which you can click on the disk defragmenter option.

Once done, you can restart your computer because you have just removed the clutter and added to its efficiency.
