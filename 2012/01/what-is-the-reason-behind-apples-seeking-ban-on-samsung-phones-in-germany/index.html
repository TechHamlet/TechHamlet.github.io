What Is the Reason Behind Apple’s Seeking Ban on Samsung Phones in Germany?

Jan 31st, 2012 | Comments

Apple has filed another patent infringement lawsuit against Samsung in Dusseldorf Regional Court, Germany. These latest lawsuits (LG Dusseldorf, 14c O 293/11 and 14c O 294/11) calls for a ban on 10 smartphones of Samsung on the ground of violation in design rights. Apple has specially mentioned about Galaxy S II, Galaxy S Plus along with that and eight other models of Samsung in that petition. In addition to this Apple has also filed a suit against five Samsung tablets. Such suits are believed to be inspired by the ban imposed on sales of Galaxy Tab 10.1 in Germany.

This legal battle is a part of the ongoing tussle between the electronic manufacturing giants over establishing leadership in sophisticated handheld electronic gadgets like smartphones and tablets. It will be interesting to look back at last few legal battles between these two corporations to understand the rationale behind such suits.

Why Samsung?

In April 2011, Samsung became the latest among growing list of technology companies that get entangled with Apple in legal battles. Samsung joined the list with Nokia, HTC and Motorola who have been entangled with Apple Inc over several patent issues ranging from software to design of smartphones.

Market observers say that recent lawsuits against Samsung show that Apple is increasingly feeling insecure over the growing sales of Android driven smartphones and tablets of Samsung. Among the several vendors who have flooded the market of Android run smartphone, Samsung is arguably the leader. Samsung’s Android based Galaxy Tab has come up as a viable alternative to iPad. Obviously, Apple has singled out Samsung as its main rival in the market.

Areas of concern

In March 2011, Steve Jobs, then CEO of Apple, while unveiling iPad2, made a jab at Samsung. He said that his company is competing with “copycat tablet makers” in 2011. It is interesting to note that such attack came at a time when Apple was sourcing its components like screens and chips and Samsung was one of its prime suppliers.

There are other areas where Apple is concerned. Samsung has dual strength – in manufacturing parts as well as finished products. Even the processors of Apple – A4 and A5 are manufactured by Samsung. The company also supplies Apple LCDs and flash memory chips. Even such close business relation has not stopped Apple from filing legal suits against Samsung. Obviously, there are bigger interests at stake.

Inspiration

In September 2011, Düsseldorf Higher Regional Court, Germany, ordered an injunction on sales of Samsung Tab across European Union on ground of infringement in patent right of design. According to Apple, Samsung Galaxy Tab has slavishly copied the look and feel of iPad. The German court without giving an opportunity of hearing to Samsung imposed the ban on sell of Galaxy Tab 10.1 across EU except Netherlands. Samsung came up with Galaxy Tab 10.1N which was a modified design of Galaxy Tab 10.1 to bypass the decision.

Conclusion

The ongoing legal battle between Apple and Samsung has intensified with Apple seeking ban on sales of Galaxy Tab 10.1N in European Union. Observers and market strategists believe that Apple is trying to entangle Samsung in legal battles and delaying launch of its products. The strategy has proved effective in many cases and probably a reason behind Apple’s legal wars against Samsung.
