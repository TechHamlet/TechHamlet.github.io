Turn Your Simple Home Router Into a Super Routing Machine!

Jan 16th, 2012 | Comments

Most of you buy a router to connect your network to the Internet. Most of the simple home routers does this well. But they don’t have many functions… Your freedom is limited by the router’s inbuilt software (we call it the firmware). But what if we can install a new firmware? Just like we format our Windows machine and install Linux?

Yep… We can do it! In the past week I lost my old router because of lightning. I always wanted to install a custom firmware in my router but it cannot be done with the old one. But its possible now…

What will I get by installing a new firmware?

A new custom firmware can give you a lot of options to twist and tweak your network than the factory installed one. Your router can do more stuff than you can imagine! You just have to give it the right software… A custom firmware might also give you more security options. For example, the DD-WRT firmware which we are going to try today have a very strong and extremely configurable firewall based on iptablesÂ (which we use on Linux). You know how hard the Linux firewall is! Not only that, there are more things like increasing the strength of the WiFi signal, overclock the router, add IPv6 support, add your own DNS server, control the QoS of your network, make your own WiFi hotspot, etc…

What should I know before doing this?

You should know 2 important facts before you flash your router…

  1. Just like rooting an Android phone, flashing the router with a custom firmware voids its warranty. But if you can put the factory firmware back before claiming warranty, you might get lucky! But don’t count on it!

  2. There’s a chance that you might lose your router! So, make sure you have the insurance!

What should I have before flashing?

  • The most important thing you need to have is patience! If you don’t have it, don’t even think about flashing the router and living to tell the tale!

  • You need a computer connected to the router with a network cable. Do not use a machine which is connected through WiFi!

Whats DD-WRT?

DD-WRT is a Linux-based custom firmware built for wireless routers. Its capable of adding a lot of features to your small router without spending more money. The best thing is, its open source and you can edit it anyway you want!

Before you start, keep in mind…

  • Always do a 30-30-30 hard reset before you begin!Â That means, keep the reset button of the router pushed for 30 seconds and turn off the power (don’t release the reset button… keep it pushed) and keep it for another 30 seconds… Again put Â the power back (without releasing the button) and keep it for another 30 seconds and release!

  • Never use Google Chrome for this job… People recommend IE or Safari. But both of them failed for me. I used Firefox…

Ok, let’s do this…

I’m not going to give you step by step instructions because each router have its own way. You must follow the hardware specific instructions! So, head over to this link and pick your device to get the instructions :Â http://www.dd-wrt.com/wiki/index.php/Hardware-specific

Warning :Â It might look like its taking a lot of time… If that’s the case, don’t touch it for about 10 minutes (The recommended time is 5 mins but better wait 10 to be sure…)! If the router’s default IP is something other than 192.168.1.1 then it won’t show anything because after updating, the default IP change to 192.1681.1 So Â if its different earlier, the browser won’t be able to connect to the router. If that happens, restart the router and things should work again!

If anything goes wrong, don’t panic like I did… Simply, take some time and think… Go to DD-WRT forums and try to find a solution… Search on Google… Leave a comment on this post and we’ll try our best to find you a solution… If it succeed, leave a comment and let us know what you think about your new router!
