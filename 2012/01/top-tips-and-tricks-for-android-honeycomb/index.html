Top Tips and Tricks for Android Honeycomb

Jan 19th, 2012 | Comments

Last year was the year of the Android tablet with dozens of models being released from a range of manufacturers including the Asus Eee Pad, the Acer Iconia Tab, the HTC Flyer, the Motorola Xoom and the Samsung Galaxy Tab.   Apple’s iPad still remains top-dog in terms of sales volume; however a few Android tablets have emerged as strong contenders, each offering their own unique selling points.

Whichever make or model you own, this Guest Post provides the top five tips and tricks to help you use your Android Honeycomb tablet faster and more smartly. 

Run Apps in Full Screen

While having a huge screen is one of the main advantages of owning a tablet, this does mean that Android Apps designed for smartphones will not display properly. Fortunately there is a solution. Go to the Android Market and locate an App called Spare Parts. Once you have downloaded and installed the App, you will need to open it and go to Compatibility Mode. Then Uncheck it. Then re-check it (so the tick appears again). Next, turn your tablet off and then back on again.

Once that is done, open the Spare Parts App again and Uncheck the compatibility box again. This time, however, do NOT re-check it.

Then restart your tablet once again. Once it has rebooted, open Spare Parts again. If the App opens in full-screen mode, then it has worked successfully. You should now be able to run any Android App which will display in full screen mode.

Browse in Full Screen

Another way to increase your screen real-estate is to hide the URL / Tab bar at the top of your Browser App. This will allow websites to be viewed in truly full screen mode and looks great.

To enable this, open the Browser App and go to Menu – Settings – Labs. Then put a check mark under Quick controls. Don’t worry though, the URL bar is not completely gone. To find it, slide a finger from off the edge of the screen towards the centre of the screen. A semi-circular menu will appear allowing you to choose various options, including showing the URL bar.

Enable Flash on Websites

For some reason many tablets running Android Honeycomb have Flash disabled when you are browsing websites. To enable Flash again, open your browser and go to Settings. Then select Advanced - Enable plug-ins. Then select Always On.

Lock Screen While Recording Videos

If you are watching a movie or video, especially if you have kids around, it is useful to lock the screen orientation and buttons so you do not interrupt your content. To do this, simply press the Power/Lock button.

Most people aren’t aware that you can also do this while recording videos. This is useful if you are a bit clumsy and are liable to pressing buttons by accident. Just start video recording and press the Power/Lock button and your recording will not be disturbed.

Read Google Books Offline

Many people purchase a tablet in order to read books, and Google Books offers a great App for this purpose. By default however, it does require you to have an active internet connection while reading the book. Luckily there is a way to read books offline without a connection.

Go to the Google Books App and locate a Book that you would like to read offline. Then press and hold the book’s cover and choose ‘Make available offline’. You can also choose other books by pressing the tacks beneath each book. Once you have finished, press Done in the bottom-right corner.
