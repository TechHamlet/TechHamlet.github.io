What Is Mobile Broadband?

Jan 26th, 2012 | Comments

Mobile broadband is a stirring latest technology, which even delivers 3G mobile internet. With the help of a data card or USB modem gadget, you can access internet from your laptop at broadband speeds of about 7.2mbs/second.

Corporate executives and mobile professionals are the typical users of the mobile broadband services. It allows users to access internet wherever they want and at any time. Now, students and parents are also taking advantage of the available mobile internet services to manage their work.

Laptops have been majority endpoints for mobile broadband services, but smartphones are complementing them and also many users are replacing the laptops.

The 3G services used here mainly comes with 2 necessary technologies: HSPDA for downloading at higher speed and HSUPA for upload packet access at higher speed. Demand for the mobile broadband service has been increasing as numerous institutions have selected this service to offer high speed internet access and connectivity, when compared to fixed line infrastructure. The best widespread method for delivering mobile broadband is by using 3GPP (3rd Generation Partnership Project) that serves the largest mobile subscriber base.

Some of the gadgets, which offer mobile networks, include PC data cards, USB modems and sticks, compact flash; data modem enabled phones, smartphones, MiFi, SDIO, and portable devices with inbuilt mobile networks support like net books, notebooks, and mobile internet devices.

There are several companies, which offer mobile broadband services; you should consider some factors while selecting mobile broadband and its service providers. The first thing is that you must consider the download speed and its allowance; next the price, mobile broadband coverage, and length of contract. Further, you should look for the technology to get increased speed, compact and smaller modems, and technologies, which boost reception.

By using mobile broadband internet service, you can access

  • Your email

  • Facebook

  • Do work research

  • YouTube

  • Streaming music and many more

The mobile broadband helps in cutting down your monthly internet charges; conventional services, which rely on the line rentals charge extra costs, while mobile broadband doesn’t require the line rentals, which can save a considerable amount every year. The mobile services are very simple, flexible, and easy to install. Once the dongle is plugged into your device, you can easily access internet without the need for installation of any software. Several plans are offered, which range from daily access fees to the monthly plans to select from based on the amount of internet you use.

There are also some disadvantages; for instance, data downloads and uploads will be limited, and will require high charges to pay if you exceed those limits. Downloads will usually be between 0.5GB –5GB per month, for almost all plans and 15 GB per month for costly plans. You may also experience slower internet access and will be able to access in areas, which will be covered by 3G network. But, new 4G technologies like WiMax, and LTE broadband will have the potential to significantly increase speeds.
