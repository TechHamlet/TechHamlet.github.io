Technology Any Home Needs

Nov 20th, 2012 | Comments

Whilst we can often focus on the very latest and flashy pieces of technology, there are plenty of vital pieces of technology out there that are simply needed in the home.

To this end, here is a list of vital technology any home should have. You might even be thinking of getting rid of some of these, but hopefully there are a few better reasons not to.

The Phone Line

A lot of people don’t use it much, but the humble phone line is still a vital part to any home. First of all, it’s the connection for any internet. If you want broadband in your home (and the chances are you have a lot of internet-hungry devices) then you need a phone connection.

Secondly, spare a thought for simple BT Phones. We may have mobile phones, but making a simple house call is still one of the cheaper options available, and the end result is the same.

Computer

In the age of smartphones and tablets, it can be difficult to remember the computer at times. You may think it can be replaced, but the computer is still the de facto device for many people.

First of all, the computer provides a great place to store things. Whilst smartphones and devices have plenty of uses, they are often limited in space, where as computers can be upgraded if needed (or you can just expand with external hard drives).

Additionally, if you use other devices, interconnectivity is very useful these days. If you want to share media files, such as music and movies, you’re going to need somewhere to store them. The computer, then, has moved from the main internet browser to a make-shift home hub for all your networked devices.

Television

Similar to the computer, you might question the current need for a TV. Yet, with smart TVs, the TV has managed to update itself to suit your modern needs. With the ability to record TV, pause live programmes, or simply catch up on missed shows, the modern smart TV brings many of today’s functions to the television. This grants you all the usual benefits from the comfort of your own living room. Watching TV on the computer is never comfortable, and smart devices have small screens. A large smart TV, however, offers the best of both worlds.

Smartphone

Whilst it’s not a necessity, the greater influence of interconnectivity is transforming the smartphone. Your average modern phone can already do a lot, but with many devices, from your computer to your TV becoming ‘smart’ and offering similar connectivity, the average smartphone is becoming the modern remote control.

This type of solution is already been shown in TVs, with smart phones being used as a remote. Even Microsoft’s Xbox 360 has unveiled similar technology. A universal remote for the entire home, the smart phone is set to become the pride of place in terms of home technology.
