The Best Phone Systems on the Market

Nov 28th, 2012 | Comments

Phone Systems

When you are trying to organise the telecoms hub of your business, it’s easy to become confused. Asking the experts only seems to get you bogged down in techno-speak and jargon; drowning in abbreviations and acronyms. However, if you want to set your office up with the best telephone systems on the market, then it is nigh-on unavoidable to dip your toe into this technical language.

Preparation keeps confusion to a minimum

The good news is that most of it can be avoided, if you come prepared and know what you want. Modern telephone systems offer a wide range of features – some as standard, others as options – such as web-conferencing, instant messaging, remote working and free calls between business hubs. Your first task is to ask yourself what you want from your office telephone network and then to look at which systems on the market can best provide you with this.

As IT and telecoms converge…

Digital phone networks have come on in leaps and bounds in the past decade in terms of quality, especially broadband-based networks. Now voice packets can be transmitted just the same way as data packets with no reduction in sound quality. We’ve all heard of Skype – well, IP (internet protocol) technology is what makes this marvel of the age possible, and now calls over the internet are also a big part of modern business telephony. Essentially, IT and telecoms are becoming more and more the same thing, with many telephone handsets offering voice, video and data services as part of the same package.

So what’s on offer?

The options out there are dependent on how big your business is and its requirements. For single-site businesses with largely office-bound staff, TDM_(Time Division Multiplexing) is a good choice. Handsets are based on circuit-switched technology, with calls carried over Public Switched Telephone Networks (PSTN). Hybrid-IP s_ystems use both TDM and IP technology, and are handy for an enterprise with a head office overseeing smaller sites and remote workers. For large national, or international businesses, then pure IP systems are ideal, since combining voice and data packages on one network can substantially reduce phone bills.

The wonders of VoIP

The major advances in Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) technology are what make this possible, enabling calls to be sent via broadband internet or a wide area network (WAN). Because voice data is compressed and sent over the internet rather than via a traditional telephone exchange, up to 90% less bandwidth is used. This is what makes it possible for a business to make long-distance and international calls for the price of local calls, free calls within a business plus the option of picking up and listening to email over the phone.
