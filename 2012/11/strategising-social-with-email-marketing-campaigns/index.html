Strategising Social With Email Marketing Campaigns

Nov 28th, 2012 | Comments

The Internet is now one of the most popular and efficient methods of marketing. The term “viral campaign” has been bandied about quite a bit lately and sees the Internet used as a vehicle to advertise through interactive marketing. Emails are sent to subscribers, social media updates are distributed, Twitter feeds are filled with the latest URL links to updated web pages, latest news stories and comments, all relating to the product/service which is being sold. With so many online mediums to make use of, how can you ensure that your marketing strategy has the right balance between social media marketing techniques and email marketing campaigns?

Social Marketing

One of the basics of online marketing is to ensure that you have a recognisable, consistent brand for your online communications. Facebook pages can be customised and “branded”, as can Twitter profiles. By ensuring that your company colours, logo and brand are consistent on your social sites as they are with your website, you are showing a high level of professionalism and increasing your chance of your brand being remembered by online visitors. You can even ensure that you obtain the relevant URLs from each social media provider to ensure that your organisation is consistent with its online presence; i.e. on Twitter, check to see if www.Twitter.com/yourcompany is available, then ensure that this is available for your Facebook profile, group or page. One of the most common mistakes is to mix up your URL formatting which can confuse and alienate online customers.

For example, your website URL might be: www.your-company.com, whereas your Facebook might be: www.facebook.com/yourcompany and your Twitter might be www.twitter.com/your_company. By checking the availability on the most common social media sites you can ensure that you have a common URL for all of your online presence.

Email Marketing

Again, with the effective use of email templates you can ensure that all of your email marketing is done with your branding via HTML email content. Your company logo and colours can adorn all emails sent through your email marketing plan to increase your chances of subscribers recalling your organisation and its services when your logo is seen elsewhere.

Email marketing needs to be effectively integrated into your social marketing plans. Social media is a great method of obtaining subscribers to email newsletters for your organisation, so you don’t want to waste these subscriptions with poor email content. Use your social media profiling to target individuals for email content, only sending relevant emails to specific demographics of your subscription list can also help. (I.e. target boys and girls under the age of 30 on your subscription list if you’re sending emails about video games).

Your email templates should always include return links back to your social network locations, but also options to “share” your email content on the social network. The best marketing is the one which comes free. If your customers are encouraged to share your email content you can gain limitless free advertising via the “have you seen this? Click here!” generation.
