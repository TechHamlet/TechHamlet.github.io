Diving Deep Into Google Drive and Comparing With the Competition

Apr 25th, 2012 | Comments

Yesterday Google released their new cloud storage service Google Drive. You can easily sync your data with all your devices with Google Drive. You can also access them online and even edit some files like documents and spreadsheets from Google Docs! Today I’m going to dive deeper into this new service and make things easier for you to choose!

Google Drive works just like Dropbox and Sky Drive. You download the software and place the special folder in your machines. What ever you put into that folder will be automatically synced between all your devices. Even without the software, you can access them through the online interface. That’s the basic idea of these kinds of tools.

How can we compare it with Dropbox and Sky Drive?

First thing I want is the platform support :

Currently Google Drive supports Mac, Windows and Android only. Linux and iOS support will be added soon. Sky Drive have Windows, Mac, iOS and Windows Phone support. I don’t think they’ll release a Linux version. But Dropbox is the one who have the widest support. It supports Linux, Windows, Mac, iOS, Android, and even Black Berry! In here, Dropbox will become a challenge to Google. They should support more platforms if they want to keep up.

Next comes the free space and price:

Google Drive offers 5GB of free space. But, no option to refer friends and increase it like Dropbox. In Dropbox they give only 2GB but you can increase it by referring people and testing their software. Sky Drive offers the highest amount of free space. They offer 7GB storage and 25GB for old users. When it comes to pricing, Sky Drive is the cheapest followed a bit closely by Google Drive. But Dropbox is a bit expensive than both of them.

How about syncing and other features?

If you put any file to Sky Drive or Dropbox, in that folder, they will add a small icon to that item indicating whether its synced, syncing or un-synced. It’s a really useful feature to see what are the things that have really synced to other devices. In Google Drive, we don’t have such a feature. A big problem for me…

When syncing, its vital for me to see in which speed it operates and how long will it take to upload the files. In here, Sky Drive and Google Drive both failed to give the relevant information. Sky Drive at least shows how many megabytes are left to upload. Google Drive shows only how many files to upload. In here, Google have to improve. When it comes to Dropbox, it shows the upload speed and the remaining amount of time. I think it’s also faster than both the others. It can even pick the changed files faster. When I put something into the folders, both Sky and Google Drives took some time to process the changes while Dropbox immediately picked up the changes and started to upload them.

Does they effect the browsing speed?

Sky Drive and Dropbox both manage the bandwidth well. They won’t let you think that they are working. The browsing won’t be effected. But Google Drive have a slight effect. We can avoid these kinds of problems if they allowed us to limit the bandwidth. But, Google Drive and Sky Drive don’t have this feature. Dropbox allows us to limit both the upload and the download speeds.

Online editing?

Google Drive and Sky Drive both have their own online office suits. Microsoft have some of the MS Office services available online for free. Google have Google Docs. So, their Drives allows us to edit documents online. But Dropbox doesn’t have this feature.

What about security and privacy?

When it comes to security, according to what I managed to dig up, none of these services encrypt our data online. There are some other services out of these which provide better security options. When compared to them, these people are doing nothing!

When it comes to privacy, you must pay more attention. Dropbox says that the ownership of the files we upload stays with us. But they ask for right to use them only to run their service. Sky Drive also gives a similar policy. When it comes to Google, they say that we own our data but they also say…

When you upload or otherwise submit content to our Services, you give Google (and those we work with) a worldwide licence to use, host, store, reproduce, modify, create derivative works (such as those resulting from translations, adaptations or other changes that we make so that your content works better with our Services), communicate, publish, publicly perform, publicly display and distribute such content.

Read it carefully… If you understand it, you’ll start to sweat… Google have the right to do anything with our data and the worst thing is, it’s not only Google… They specifically say “Google (and those we work with)”! That’s dangerous! Really really dangerous! That’s where I draw the line!

The verdict…

Google is a very reputable company and they know how to conquer the world. Unlike Yahoo, they know how to go into every field and be the king. Google Drive offers a good amount of features and it’s really simple. But, there are things missing… For the start, I really want support for Linux and clear identification of synced and non synced items. I also need to know how long will it take to upload my data. So, I think Google should be given more time to adjust.

When it comes to the privacy policy, I jump away from Google Drive like I got an electric shock. I think the best service for me is Dropbox. Simple, Fast, and they know how to do the job really well. There are many services which offer more space and security than Dropbox. But there’s a reason for it to come to the very top. Its simplicity and control. It have what I need and I’ll stay with it. I’ll also keep a close eye on Google Drive! What do you think? What’s your choice?
