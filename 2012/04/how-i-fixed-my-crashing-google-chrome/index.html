How I Fixed My Crashing Google Chrome

Apr 8th, 2012 | Comments

I was really angry because of Google Chrome’s bad habit of crashing my whole machine. In the past months, I suffered a lot from this problem. Most of the time it gets stuck and I have to force the machine to shut down. It takes the whole machine with it. Some times, it made my VGA to crash.

I was furious… I updated my drivers… Made a clean install of the VGA drivers… Formatted the machine… Took the laptop to the HP service center twice… First they replaced my hard drive because of bad sectors. But still the problem is there. Next they said the machine is fine and there’s nothing wrong with it!

So, I took the machine home and did a lot of research. Many have recommended to disable hardware acceleration in Flash to resolve this issue. I’m not sure about this fix but I applied it. Upto now, my browser didn’t got stuck! My VGA crashed 2 times but no deadly crashes for nearly a month! That’s a big relief!

So, How to apply this fix?

Now we need to disable Hardware Acceleration in Flash. To do this, we need to go to a site which have flash content and adjust the settings.

  • First, go here : http://get.adobe.com/flashplayer/ , right-click on the flash banner and click on Settings as shown below :

  • Now in the settings box, un-tick Enable hardware acceleration option and click Close :

Now you have disable the hardware acceleration feature in flash. This might reduce the quality of flash videos a bit. But you might not notice it. Also you don’t have to worry about it much because the future is HTML5… Not Flash!

Did it fix your issue?

Most of the time it will fix your problem. Some times it won’t. Why? Because Flash is not the only thing which can make the browser crash. Scripts, malware, hardware failures, drivers conflicts and many more things can contribute to it. Some people even blame Microsoft. Some say that our graphics drivers fail to work with Chrome because of a bug in Windows. Im not so sure about it but in my Linux machine, I run Chrome without any problem! Update : Does your browser crash when you close it or close any tab?

Our friend Niroth also had the same problem and he have successfully fixed it by disabling the background apps :

My chrome used to crash whenever i closed chrome (by the tab). So to fix that problem go to “settings” –> “under the hood” and disable “background apps”. Now my chrome works fine.
