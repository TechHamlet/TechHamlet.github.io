Linux, Windows : The Junk Man Is Here to Clean Your Systems

Dec 2nd, 2009 | Comments

Why do we clean our systems? One, to keep it healthy… two, to ensure privacy! We have lots of free and payed tools which will help us to maintain a junk free system. Actually they does a pretty good job, but most of them are built only for Windows! But today I found a great Janitor which cleans both Linux and Windows systems…….

Do you know that when you use some programs, they make temporary files to help them work. Most of the programs don’t remove these files correctly so they pile up and ultimately slows down your system!

Do you know that your browser stores the user-names and passwords you use in your browser (for example your e-mail account’s password) as cookies? And do you know that a spy-ware can easily access these cookies and get your passwords to hack into your accounts or send you Spam?

Now this is why we should clean our systems. BleachBit is a great free tool which cleans your systems and ensure the privacy and good performance of your system! The greatest feature I see in bleach bit is that it is also available for Linux users. This tool quickly:

  • Frees disk space

  • Removes hidden junk

  • Erase cache

  • Delete cookies

  • Clear Internet history

  • Remove unused localizations

  • Shred logs

  • Delete temporary files.

  • Wipes clean 70 applications including Firefox, Internet Explorer, Flash, Google Chrome, Opera, Safari, Adobe Reader, APT, and more.

Other than simply deleting files, BleachBit includes advanced features such as shredding files to prevent recovery, wiping free disk space to hide traces of files deleted by other applications, and vacuuming Firefox to make it faster.

Interested? Then, how to get this great tool?

  • You can download the latest version of BleachBit from here.

  • If you are using Ubuntu 9.10, things are a bit tricky…. Some times the latest version of BleachBit won’t work on your system, but don’t worry… we can make it work! Heres how to do it :

1) First we need to install the old version of BleachBit from the Ubuntu repositories. To do that open the terminal and enter the following :

sudo apt-get install bleachbit

2) Next download the latest “deb” package from the BleachBit website and install it.
