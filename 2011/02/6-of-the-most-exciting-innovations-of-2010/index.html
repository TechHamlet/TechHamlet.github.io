6 of the Most Exciting Innovations of 2010

Feb 19th, 2011 | Comments

2010 is in the past, but there were a lot of exciting innovations that warrant a reflection. From gadgets to applications and more, these innovations allowed consumers and companies to do more and experience more. Life was made easier by many of these innovations, and we can all look forward to what the next year brings us in the way of technology.

Most people carry their phones with them no matter where they go, and this innovation is perfect if you have forgotten your wallet. You can simply utilize your phone to pay for your items. Not every store has this feature, but it is available at Starbucks and a few other places. Hopefully we see this innovation available at all retail stores in the future.

  1. Mobile payment: Mobile payment is an exciting innovation that allows a consumer to pay for an item with their smart phones. No longer do you have to stand in line and try to find a credit card in your wallet.

  2. iPad: What started out as an innovation that had many consumers skeptical became a must-have item of 2010. Consumers first worried about the iPad since it couldn’t even make a phone call, but before long, it was one of the top-selling items of 2010 and had the crowds waiting in line for hours just to get one. Companies can even set up virtualization on the iPad and communicate with their employees without being in the same room.

  3. Google’s self-driving car: When we think of cars that drive themselves, perhaps a futuristic sci-fi movie or The Jetsonâ€™s come to mind. However, in 2010, Google created the self-driving automobile. Even though it may be quite some time before it’s available to consumers, it’s an innovation that is light years ahead of the competition.

  4. Sony Bloggie Camera: The Bloggie camera is an awesome little HD camera with a touchscreen. Small enough to put in your pocket- powerful enough to shoot the video (and take the pictures) you need.

  5. Insect repellent travel chair: Made with an insect repellent fabric, this chair allows you to go anywhere and not have to worry about insects bothering you. Whether it’s watching a football game or a camping trip in the forest, this chair keeps the pests away.

  6. HP Photo Smart All in One Printer: This smart printer allows a user to print wirelessly and doesn’t require a computer to let it know what to do next. It has a handy touch screen where everything is inclusive. This makes printing on the go much easier to do.

As you can see, 2010 had plenty of exciting and helpful innovations. While some aren’t fully in use yet, all of these innovations are worth the wait, and each one allows the user to be helped in a variety of ways.

What is even more exciting is that it doesn’t matter if you’re the CEO of a company or a stay-at-home mom, because these innovations have something for everyone to enjoy and find helpful. What products are on your list of most exciting innovations of 2010?
