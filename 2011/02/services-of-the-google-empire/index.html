Services of the Google Empire

Feb 21st, 2011 | Comments

Google the ultimate search engine offers more than just search engine and email. It has a wide array of important services to any user whether you are a end user or a developer. To cut a long story short, I’ll list the services and describe them by categorizing the services into Developer tools and End User tools. That would cut of painful redundancy.

End User Tools

  • Search Engine – The most commonly known to the grassroots level. (Food for thought – The new proverb has emerged punning the old saying – “Google before you Tweet”)

  • Gmail – The most used email provider of the world that offers many facilities. It has the best know spam filter and I have detailed a post about the Spam Filter recently at my Blog

  • Google Buzz – A macro blogging service like Twitter that is popular among the Gmail users

  • YouTube – Google owns the world’s largest video house. YouTube currently holds the Biggest community for Video lovers. It is a great service to become a celebrity too. (example- Justin Bieber)

  • iGoogle – The service that can be used to customize the Google simple interface into a complex dashboard with many widgets that you love.

  • Google Maps – The best map service available for use and has satellite integration included into it

  • Google Reader – After subscribing to RSS feed from a blog or a website you can use Reader to read the post.

  • Google Profile – Profile is a great way of maintaining your internet identity. This will also be used for Google Buzz

  • Google Docs – A handy way of backing up your Documents and editing them online. You can use it even as a public showcase by using the URL to the document

  • Google Calendar – A comprehensive way of managing and scheduling time. Non Google alternatives are rememberthemilk and todo.ly

  • Blogger – A pain free tool to make blogs. The blogger can worry about content more than the management of the blog. But the user control and customization is inhibited unlike Wordpress package

  • Picasa Web Albums – An alternative for Filcker. We can easily incorporate it with Gmail so don’t waste the free web space Google grant for you.

  • Google Book Search – Search for thousands of textual material in the internet and read most of them for free. For some books only previews are available

  • Google Translator – A great tool if you ever come across a foreign web site that holds valuable information. It has support for many languages too

  • Google Groups – A easy way to make groups and discuss issues related to the particular niche

  • Google Checkout – A payment supporting service like PayPal that safely manage the important information

  • Google News – Being updated had never been easy. With Google news you can get the latest  updates about many fields

Developer Tools

  • Google Analytics – Google’s way of analyzing the traffic to page and it offers many options

  • Google App Engine – Hosting your web application on Google App Engine allows developers for faster development and deployment

  • Google Webmaster tools – Tools for you to maximize the visibility of your website on Google. You can upload your website’s site map to make things easy. You can also link Webmaster’s tools to Analytics account

  • Google Code Search – Search for thousands of scripts and other useful codes that can be reused in our web applications

  • Google Ajax libraries API – Use this library to build Ajax applications easily

  • FeedBurner – With FeedBurner you can easily send RSS to your subscribers and FeedBurner allows you to monetize you feeds

  • Google AdSense/ Adwords – The two counter parts of the Google’s advertising network. For publishers AdSense is the best option to earn money from a website and for Advertisers Adwords is the easiest way to get many visitors for you web application

  • Google Trends– Find the trends in your country and your niche and write about them or develop applications for the demand. It’s a great way of achieving mass profit from applications

There are many services hidden inside Google too. But the above list featured the most used tools by end users and developers. If you haven’t tried a service yet please go ahead and test it. Comment about your experience about Google products. Another tool used by many people I failed to mentioned on my list is Google Chrome. The browser I personally use. The Chrome App store can be used to covert the Web experience to a whole different level.
