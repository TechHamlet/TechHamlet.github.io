Facebook Is Rolling Out a New Fan Page Design!

Feb 11th, 2011 | Comments

Recently, Facebook released a new re-design to its profile pages. Today, they are rolling out another re-design to their fan pages! This latest design will turn the fan pages to look like the normal profiles. This is not only a simple redesign, the new Fan page is going to come with a load of new features which most of the page admins would love to use!

As I said earlier, the new fan page design have a lot copied from the profiles. For example, the addition of recently tagged photos at the top of the page, A quick bio, Removal of the tabs and moving the links to the left are some of those copied changes. This will give the users the same experience in the profiles and the fan pages.

Earlier, the wall of a page will show only the recent posts. But, the new page will also show the posts from the page and the top posts from everyone else! Normally, we have filters in the wall to filter out the posts. The new design will add some special filters for the admins under the admin view.

One of the biggest features is the addition of “Use Facebook as Your Page” feature. Normally, the pages don’t have some features the normal user does. But, now some of the features are coming to the fan page little by little. If you wish to use Facebook as your Page, that means, your profile is the Fan Page. Got it? You can get notifications when your fans interact with your pages or the posts, like other pages just like a normal user, leave comments using your own page and more cool features are there in the new “Use Facebook as Your Page” concept.

Also there are more changes in the “Edit Settings” page. Now you can set Featured Likes (Pages that you like most) and Featured Admins (The best admins of your page) through the edit settings page. Earlier, if you are the owner of a page, when you post on that page, the posts will be posted under the name of your page. Your name won’t be shown. But, now you can change this. You can also add profanity filters and also completely delete the page if you don’t need it anymore!

These are only a small amount of changes. There are a lot of new features coming with the new Fan Page design. Currently, Facebook is on the process of rolling out the new design. If your lucky, you’ll get a notification on the top of the page asking you to try out the new design. But, don’t worry, Facebook will make this new design default for everyone on the beginning on March!
