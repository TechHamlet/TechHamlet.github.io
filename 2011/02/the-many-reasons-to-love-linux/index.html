The Many Reasons to Love Linux

Feb 28th, 2011 | Comments

Most people don’t want to know much about their computers.  As long as they boot up and provide emails and word docs, the operating system is unimportant.  But that’s most people.  If you’re tired of the tyranny of Microsoft and Apple (having to purchase expensive software every time you get a new computer or the latest version comes out for upgrade), then perhaps it’s time you took a more active role in understanding your technology.  By operating your machines on Linux, you stand to gain quite a bit.

One of the first things you should know about Linux is that it’s open source (to the layman that means free, although it can be sold, along with supplementary software).  It’s a UNIX-based system that is extremely flexible and can be used with nearly all of your technological gadgets.  It is not only compatible with a wide variety of computer hardware, but it can also run on everyday devices like video-game consoles, tablets, and cell phones.  That means you could have all of your devices running on the same platform.  And if that’s not enough, it might interest you to know that the top supercomputers in the world run on Linux.  In fact, it has been lauded by people in the know as one of the best operating systems around because of its speed, stability, and lack of technical issues (no more blue screen of death).  So why don’t you have it?

The problem is that the majority of people who utilize a computer operating system have no idea how it works.  If you start talking about binary code and packets of information, you may notice that the eyes of your listener slowly glaze over.  Not only do people not understand how their technology works; they don’t want to know.  They just want to push a button and have the world at their fingertips.  It is for this convenience that they pay when they purchase operating systems created by Microsoft and Apple.  The average person simply doesn’t want to think about lines of code or computer languages.  So the thing that makes Linux special and extremely useful has also held it back.

Despite the fact that you can get the source code for free and modify it to your specifications, you actually have to know something about computer language and programming to do so.  Well, not entirely.  Like other operating systems, there are companies out there selling Linux software that has been modified and enhanced to work on your computer without you having to learn to manage it yourself.  There are also quite a few supplementary programs available that act in much the same ways as expansions for Windows, for example.  Of course, this totally undermines many of the benefits to be had by Linux, but it makes the system more accessible and user-friendly.

In the long run, you’re going to have to garner at least a basic understanding of programming language if you want to enjoy the many benefits of Linux.  But you don’t have to be some kind of computer genius to use it or to recognize its value.  More and more people are gaining knowledge of computers and how they function, especially kids who are growing up immersed in a culture of computer technology.  So if you think Linux sounds like a pretty good deal, simply pick up a beginner’s guide and see if it’s right for you.  One more benefit of Linux: you get to take control of your technology!
