5 Tips to Save Your Mobile Battery From Drowning

Jan 27th, 2011 | Comments

While many of us have started using the mobile phones regularly, the main reason for their wide usage is the portability of these devices; logically this option should use a battery power to back up the various wide ranges of features and utilities. The more the features and utilities available to a phone, the more the battery usage will be, and the more you use the phone, the lesser the life of the battery be. Still, good practices while using your battery can save your mobile battery from drowning, and I have listed here for you 5 best practices:

1. Switch off the phone when not in use:

Preferably, you will not be using your mobile phone in the night when you sleep. You can switch off your mobile phone this time. This will save on your battery usage and will make it last longer, because, the life of the battery goes down every time you recharge the battery.

2. Stop looking or signal in low coverage area:

When you find that while traveling in a low coverage area, your phone will automatically try to locate better signal transmission and in this process will use a considerable amount of battery. Therefore, in such instances, this is wise to switch the mobile phone off and this way you will find that, you are lessening the amount of battery being used. This in turn, ensures that you are doing the right thing for saving your mobile from drowning.

3. Use either the vibration alert tone or ringing tone only:

When you are using both vibration alert tone and ringing tone on your mobile phone you are increasing the usage of battery, which in turn will increase the number of recharges of your battery and will decrease the life of the battery than expected. The more the number of recharges you do to your battery the lesser the life of the battery. Therefore, when you are going to carry your mobile phone into your pocket during a meeting or in a conference, then vibration alert is much better than a ringing tone, and leave the ringing tone alone on, as we are quite used to ringing tone only.

4. Avoid using additional features unless required:

Well, if saving the life of your battery is more important than barging to others about the features of your mobile phone, when you are only a furlong away to a café to use internet, then please avoid using them, as these are the features that consumes more energy draining your battery and reducing the life of your battery. Some of the features that you can avoid usage to the maximum will include, Bluetooth, Wi-Fi, GPS, Infrared and other connectivity solutions. Forget not to the keep them switched off, when not required.

5. Use a black Background:

The best advice for most of the mobile phones having AMOLED display is to use a black background. This is because, there is a finding that AMOLED display screen uses less power in a black background than a white one. In this kind of display screen, if you browse on websites like bGoog.com, you will find that you are saving power while browsing too, on a black Google background.

6. Use One SIM Only Mobiles:

The reason not being still clear why a single SIM mobile uses lesser energy than a dual SIM phone, which by using logic, I believe, will depend on the number of times that you switch between the SIMs. This transformation is a task that will use both software and hardware changes, which might be the reason for more power consumption.  So always use single sim only mobiles for longer battery life!
