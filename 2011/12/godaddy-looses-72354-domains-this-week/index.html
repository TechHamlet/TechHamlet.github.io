GoDaddy Looses 72,354 Domains This Week

Dec 25th, 2011 | Comments

Hi guys, these days  everyone is talking about the future of the GoDaddy. This week alone, GoDaddy has lost more than 72,000 domain registrations with an yearly discount rate of about $6.99; that’s more than half a MILLION dollars per year. This figure is definitely not enough to oppose a bill.

Further more, despite the massive Twitter and blog spot campaigns heading “Go Daddy no longer supports SOPA legislation”, these figures are outstanding and highly concerned for the future of the GoDaddy.

The question at hand is that, “How many domains is the company willing to lose before they oppose this abomination of legislation?”.

Is 72,354 domains enough? Well as we all know, for GoDaddy this number is like a needle in the haystack with over 15,000 to 40,000 domains being added everyday.

It seems like GoDaddy has not taken this issue pretty seriously. The CEO’s statement even makes it more clearer.

Adelman couldn’t commit to changing its position on the record in Congress when asked about that, but said “I’ll take that back to our legislative guys, but I agree that’s an important step.” But when pressed, he said “We’re going to step back and let others take leadership roles.” He felt that the public statement removing their support would be sufficient for now, though further steps would be considered.

Do they really think that when they step back the others will take up leadership roles? To my personal experience I guess no. And even if you think at the ground levels the answer would be the same.

To give you a rough idea about what the community is talking about this I have quoted a comment I saw in a very popular site (Sorry guys mentioning the name of the site is against the Techhamlet rules ). Here’s what he says,

After hearing about the Godaddy protest, I updated my contact information on GoDaddy for my two domains. When I went to transfer the domains, I learned that my domains were locked to Godaddy until February. So, if you need to update your information before you transfer, beware! Only update the “administrative” information and not the technical or other categories.

This is just ONE person. How much more are out there?

The time has come for GoDaddy to take ACTION.
