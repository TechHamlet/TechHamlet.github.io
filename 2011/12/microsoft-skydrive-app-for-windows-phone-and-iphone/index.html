Microsoft SkyDrive App for Windows Phone and iPhone

Dec 24th, 2011 | Comments

SkyDrive is a file hosting service. It is like a mini photo album and a concise briefcase containing your important files and memorable photographs. It is part of the Microsoft family and requires your Windows Live ID to access it. This helps you to control access to your files either making it private or sharing it with your friends. It offers 25 GB of storage space with 100MB of free space for personal storage.

SkyDrive for Windows Phone and iPhone

Microsoft recently released SkyDrive for Windows phone and iPhone. This application would be especially useful for those who already use SkyDrive. Therefore, they can access their files on the go and can even upload their pictures despite the location. Such applications have become a requirement in today’s fast paced life. In one of the blogs by Microsoft, Mike Torres stated that achieving the complete, wholesome internet experience is a conundrum. To make things simpler and convenient, people want to go for devices that are handy, easily accessible and compatible. Hence, Microsoft has made it their aim to improve SkyDrive so its users can have that wholesome experience.

iPhone’s SkyDrive

The iPhone version is available in 32 languages. However, the interface is the same providing access to your SkyDrive folders, enabling you to share links of files and folders, and helping you to delete and create folders via the application. Torres, regarding this, said in his blog that with the iPhone, they have added in more convenience by inserting a 32 language feature that will ensure a worldwide usage. Furthermore, by only using their Mail App, iPhone users can now delete, create, upload and share just like on a pc. To share files, you just need to choose any file or folder you want and share it with as many people you like.

Microsoft introducing SkyDrive app on iPhone proves the fact that Microsoft is aware of what most of its users are using. This is why Ian Moulster, Microsoft’s product manager stated that they wanted to encompass all devices out there in the market so that nobody is left out from enjoying the utility of SkyDrive. However, whether Android lovers will be able to use this app or not is still a mystery.

SkyDrive for Windows Phone

You can even download other applications such as Microsoft Office for better integration. It even allows you to edit excel documents unlike iOS. When comparing SkyDrive in windows phone with iCloud, the windows SkyDrive supersedes as you can use other Microsoft apps with it. Whereas Apple’s iCloud  is not developed enough open the files in the phone in the conventional way, it offers syncing options and Photo Stream that caters to your photo organization. On the other hand, SkyDrive for Windows Phone offers exactly the same features too.

 Microsoft expects SkyDrive app to become the same sensation as skydrive.com. This has been evolved over the years so that it becomes everybody’s favorite sharing app. Let’s hope to see this app reach every available device soon.
