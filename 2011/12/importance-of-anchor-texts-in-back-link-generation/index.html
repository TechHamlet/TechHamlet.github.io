Importance of Anchor Texts in Back-Link Generation

Dec 5th, 2011 | Comments

One can find some information from Google regarding the anchor text phrases, which are used by users’ link to their site. Recently, Google Webmaster Central has revealed a new feature. Earlier, Google already reported anchor text data with top level keywords, but not phrases. Getting backlinks for keywords with single word or 2 words is currently not having much importance as it used to be earlier. Even many On-Page and White Hat SEO Software services like Sheer SEO have updated their software as per latest amendments. 

Mostly, single letter or two-letter keywords are useless data, whereas long-tail phrases are the most helpful resources that can be used to your advantage; so, here’s how a keyword-phrase will make difference. Making use of best and reliable SEO software services to keep track of our backlinks for prescribed key-phrases works really well and I consider them as best time saving elements.

Finding the Anchor Text Data

First of all, one has to be a confirmed Google Webmaster Central member to manipulate the data. If this is the first time you are verifying your domain, then you may have to wait more time to become anchor text data member.

After you’re logged in, you are free to select any site from the “My Sites” display. After that site is loaded, select the “Statistics” tag, and then choose the “Page analysis” link, and a table would appear. Using SEO Software services like Sheer SEO which is an online service provider that helps you to track your backlinks sources, keyword density, on-page optimization, your social media activity, weekly reports, SERP tracking and many exciting features. According to me Sheer SEO is one of the must have SEO software for every webmaster.

What does the Anchor Text mean? How People Use it to Link You?

Let’s consider the phrase “search engine land” to know how these anchor text phrases will work. If you are already an SEO expert, you’d already know it, but if you’re a newbie, what these anchor texts essentially show is that most of the other websites use these anchor texts to link to that website.

The point is to generate highly targeted back-links with desired anchor text; in this case, in-links with anchor texts like or as quite worthless in terms of getting boost in SERP, nonetheless they’d help in getting some PR juice. So again SEO software services like Sheer SEO comes into act.

So, start looking at anchor text data, and try to ensure that you get the right anchor-texts from sites linking to you… it may not be a bad idea to shoot an e-mail to a fellow webmaster and request him/her to alter the anchor text s/he used for linking to your site, depending upon the keyword focus on your web page – for instance, if you’re page is Top 10 SEO tips, you can ask them to link to it, by saying something like – check out WPSkool’s – Top 10 SEO Tips rather than “click here” to read the top 10 SEO tips.
