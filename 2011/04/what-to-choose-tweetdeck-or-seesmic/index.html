What to Choose TweetDeck or Seesmic?

Apr 11th, 2011 | Comments

TweetDeck and Seesmic are the leading Tweeter supportive applications built in all history. They provide the kick-butt management power to social media and they can be considered as the tools of the trade in Social Media. So what’s good? TweetDeck? Seesmic? Both of them has plus marks and negative marks. I have used both of them. I recently tried out Seesmic but it has show me miracles I haven’t seen in TweetDeck, my Twitting partner.

From a study done in 2011 we can clearly find out that 48% of Tweets are coming from unofficial Apps.

The further study shows thatÂ TweetDeck occupies around 13% in the pie chart.

When we are talking at Seesmic. It is also famous for it’s vast platform support and easy tools for the social media trade.Both interfaces are seemingly like the same. They are simple to use and they have relatively are lot of features.

What you’ll love in TweetDeck?

This question is not that easy to explain. Comparing with other twitter clients and Twitter web interface… TweetDeck is very helpful.

  • It’s simple and complex Yeah… TweetDeck is simple to Tweet and use and it’s easy to configure too. But TweetDeck also have many advance features such as changing the default font scheme to see international characters, synchronizing between multiple platforms, Changing color scheme, having function to edit retweets without directly retweeting, auto-including hashtags when replying and there are many more hidden features.

  • Vast platform support TweetDeck supports Desktop, iPhone, iPod Touch, iPad, Android, and Chrome too. Too sad it doesn’t have an app for Firefox yet. But the support seems really good. Plus you can synchronize across all these platforms by having a TweetDeck account.

  • Many Social Networks TweetDeck currently supports Google Buzz, Twitter, LinkedIn, Facebook, Foursquare, MySpace. And in the future this list will expand supporting many other services too.

  • Custom Search You can search for a hash tag by using TweetDeck’s multipanel supporting feature. Since it’s a deck you can have many columns containing many Hashtags.

  • One Click following You can easily follow any user by one click when they follow you or anyone who appears on your stream as retweets. This is great!

Only problem I had with TweetDeck was that TweetDeck for Chrome didn’t support the tab to have New Followers. Don’t know why they didn’t add it to the Chrome App. But TweetDeck is like all in one social media manager which anyone can use.

But recently I downloaded Seesmic seeing that it supports Formspring too. I wanted to check Seesmic to see if it’s worth it to spend time on configuring it. But I found many things I loved about Seesmic too.

What you’ll in Seesmic

  • It supports many other networks Specially Formspring and Tumblr. That’s great. There is a plugin market place where you can get yourself the apps you want. I also found out that it supports Ning, last.fm, and Google Reader. This is definitely something you have to look forward too.

  • Separate Decks Wow. Not like TweetDeck, Seesmic supports separate decks you can use to organize streams according to lists and many more. This function is definitely missed in TweetDeck when you get addicted to add many streams.

  • Image Previews Yes. You can preview the images shared by your friends on Seesmic. Rather than clicking a link to go to a site to view the image you can directly see it on your stream.

  • Klout score You can now see the Klout score of any person on your stream. This is great to find insight about specific people and see what that person mostly tweet about.

So what should I use?

Well that’s up to you. You can chose between TweetDeck and Seesmic in your preference
