Lets Use Evince + Ctrl + I to Protect Our Eyes While Reading E-Books!

Apr 15th, 2011 | Comments

If you are staring at your computer for a very long time, you might want to think twice about your eyes. Most people use various tricks to save their eyes. Using computer glasses, screen filters, potato slices to cool the eyes, using various software to dim the monitor…. These things really work, but remember, our eyes are very important! So, we should do everything we can to protect them.. So, here’s another trick for you to save your precious eyes!

Normally, your eyes will take a lot of energy while reading a book. But when it comes to reading an e-book in a brightly illuminated monitor, things will get more difficult! Most of the people find it hard to read the letters due to the bright white background. So, most of us might find it easier to read if you just switch the colors! Can we do this?

Of course we can. Thanks to our great open source community, now we have a great free document reader called Evince. It have one great feature which is very important for me. Its called Inverted Colors. If you activate this feature, Evince will change the colors of your document making it easier to read. For example, if you have black letters in a white background, this feature will turn your background to black and the text to white!

I don’t know about you, but this takes a huge load off my eyes. Its easier to read with this feature rather than reading in the normal mode. What do you think?

Now, how to use this feature?

You have two options :

  1. Simply press Ctrl+I in your keyboard

  2. Or, go to View > Inverted Colors

So, what do you think? Isn’t is easier to read in this way? If you have any more cool tricks on how to protect your eyes, don’t forget to share them as comments!

Download Evince (It’s available for both Windows and Linux):

  • http://live.gnome.org/Evince/Downloads

Update : Just now the inventor of this cool feature, Mr. Juanjo Marin gave us another way to use it. For easy access, you can add this tool to the toolbar :

  • Put your mouse on the toolbar, right click on it and select Toolbar

  • Next, find the Inverted Colors icon as shown below. Then, drag it and drop it in the toolbar!

**

**

You also have the option of adding a “inverting colors” button in the toolbar. Just press with the right button any blank space in the toolbar, choose “Toolbar” and DnD the button.
