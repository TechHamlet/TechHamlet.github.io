Iolo DriveScrubber Give Away (3 Keys | Worth Over $85)

Apr 14th, 2011 | Comments

Think that you have a very confidential file in your PC! You delete it. Now you are trying to give your hard drive to your friend who will do anything to get his hands on that file and blackmail you! Can he do that? Yes he can! You might think that you have permanently deleted that file… But the truth is, the data is still there! Scary isn’t it?

When you create a file, your system will store the data on your hard drive and store the information about that location (a reference) in a separate place. When you delete the file, your system will only delete the reference and forget about your data. But, your data still resides in the hard drive until your system decides to over write it with something else! So, if your friend knew this fact, then he might have already recovered your confidential file.

To solve this problem and wipe your data for good… You can get the help of iolo DriveScrubber. DriveScrubber will overwrite the deleted file several times making it really hard to recover the file. According to the government standards, you have to overwrite the file 7 times. But, DriveScrubber will allow you to overwrite the file 100 times making it impossible to recover! Like this, you can wipe your free space without harming your existing files… Cool isn’t it?

The same theory applies for most disk formats. Most of the time, when you format the disk, it will just remove the references and forget about the data. Here also you can use DriveScrubber to save your skin! Drivescrubber will let you wipe any of your drives completely without even rebooting. But if it’s the system drive, you have to reboot your machine.

DriveScrubber also allows you to create a bootable rescue disk and wipe all your drives without login into your system. So, your hard drive will look like a brand new disk! But I should give you one warning… Wiping your drive too many times might reduce the lifespan of your disk because its like scratching the disk. It’s ok to perform simple wipes occasionally and do full wipes when you really need it. So, don’t get scared!
