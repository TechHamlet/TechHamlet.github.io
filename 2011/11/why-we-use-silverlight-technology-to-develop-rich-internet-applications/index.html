Why We Use Silverlight Technology to Develop Rich Internet Applications?

Nov 21st, 2011 | Comments

Silverlight is Microsoft’s answer to Adobe Flash. With a fast enough net connection (see Charter internet services for a prime example of rapid home connections), Silverlight offers a Mac and Windows compatible tech, which allows multiple interactive functions to mesh in a single run time environment.

Microsoft is also offering to bear the cost of uploading Silverlight files to the web – meaning that small developers with something big to say can avail themselves of the technology without worrying overly about the cost.

Downloading Silverlight files is not a problem: most of the big suppliers are providing a series of Internet connection selections these days, ranging from the ultra fast to the merely respectable. A quick look, for instance, at the Charter internet service speeds reveals that you can get a perfectly adequate 1.5 MBps on download from a DSL connection – or you can move up through the ranks and enjoy speeds ranging between 12 MBps (for the Charter Express package) to the earth shattering 60 MBps that the Charter Ultra 60 package delivers.

There are several factors to consider when you choose your home or business net provider and speed is only one of them. The most important by far is availability. Let’s use a common analogy to illustrate what we mean here. The speed of your internet connection, whoever you get it from, is theoretical – that is to say, it’s a theoretical maximum, which you could get in optimum circumstances.

So the first thing to remember is that your Charter internet connection, or your AT&T connection or whatever you have, is unlikely to exist in those optimal conditions very often or for very long.

Now what was our common analogy? Think about the Internet as a highway – a real one, with cars on it. Now let’s say that your theoretical maximum download speed (the 670 MBps offered by Charter internet and its Ultra 60 package, for example) is a really fast sports car. The problem is this: if your stretch of the internet (the highway) is a little muddy track full of potholes and covered in rocks, then your super fast connection is just going to smash itself to pieces when it tries to drive down it.

In terms of choosing an internet provider, what that means is this: if the company you thought you wanted to use operates a tiny portion of the net in your area, then its maximum download speeds will crash and burn, just like the car. When you pick your internet provider you must make sure that you get a constant, strong, reliable signal.

Modern internet connections offer much more versatility and flexibility than old ones. A Charter internet connection can support TV, phone, and high speed internet in both cable and wireless forms. All the mobile devices in range of your wireless router will enjoy good connection speeds no matter how many applications they are running. And there’s a good amount of cross platform pollination between the differing applications too.

These days, your phone can send messages to your TV screen – while your internet connection manages all the streamed video content you watch on the TV screen. Wherever you are in the building, you’re connected and ready to go.
