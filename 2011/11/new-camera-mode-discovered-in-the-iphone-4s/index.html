New Camera Mode Discovered in the iPhone 4S

Nov 21st, 2011 | Comments

A new photo mode has been discovered by iPhone 4S users, hidden within iOS 5 software.

The wide photo mode is available on both the iPhone 4S and iPad cameras. The mode was discovered by iOS jail breakers, those who remove the limitations Apple impose on devices and operating systems. It opens access to hidden features or enables them to run non Apple or App store software on the device. Although legal in the US the practice can make the warranty void.

The panorama camera feature was discovered meaning it was not enabled on the iPhone 4S. A panoramic shot is a wide angle shot, usually encompassing a greater area than the camera lens can fit in one straight snap. The camera, instead, sweeps across the landscape or view, and several images are taken and stored. The camera then fits these pictures together in one picture, like a widescreen shot.

Apple has been working on a panorama camera so this discovered feature offers an insight into what iPhone users might have in store in the future. The panorama option is selected in the camera menu. Tap the camera button and then the camera is moved from left to right to capture all the images to be used.

A 3D panorama camera is a feature of the Android 2.3.4 Gingerbread upgrade across Sony Xperia handsets unveiled this year. 3D and 2D images can be taken with the 3D Panorama Sweep. Sony Ericsson is rolling out an OS upgrade for handset users which includes the new feature at the moment.

The camera has been a popular feature of the iPhone 4S, showing a marked improvement from those in previous iPhone generations. An 8 megapixel camera is a significant improvement from the 5 megapixel offer on the iPhone 4. A new sensor feature means the camera lets in more light, a feature known as next generation backside illumination. It was first introduced on the iPhone 4 but improve for the 4S. It means the camera operates much better in low light settings and responds quicker instead of needed complex changes to exposure.

Apple described the shutter speed as around a third faster on the iPhone 4S meaning images can be taken much faster in a row as well as storing the picture quicker making the camera much more responsive.; The lens is f/2.4, meaning more light hits the sensor when taking a picture with a wider focal length, an upgrade when compared to the iPhone 4. Stabilization features, vital when the device is so light are another new feature. These significant improvements are the reason the camera on the iPhone 4S is one of the best on the smartphone market, and have made it an attraction for those looking to upgrade or renew a contract. That such effort has been put into improving the product, only for a new camera feature to be discovered suggests this will continue to be an area Apple will focus on in the future.

Despite being burned by speculation this autumn, many commentators are now looking to autumn 2012 for an iPhone 5 launch. However, with a hidden feature and major work put in to improve the iPhone camera, many will look at the Panorama option as their first view of Apple’s future.
