I Have Spent 11.6 Hours on Twitter : This Is a Scam!

Mar 2nd, 2011 | Comments

Just now another big Twitter scam have started to spread through the big social network. This time, they are trying to help you find out how many hours you have spent on Twitter! Will they? No. If you click on this scam link and authorize that application to access your Twitter account, it will take control of your account and automatically use it to spread the scam!

It looks like the main suspect of this attack is an account named probleme_iphone. When I checked the search results, I found that this account have tweeted this scam message many times in a very small time… That’s not all, the account is now being suspended by Twitter!

If you have already got caught to this scam.. How to get rid of it?

  • Go to your Twitter account and click on “Settings” (In the new profile, click on your username at the top right corner and go to settings. The old profile has the settings link on the top)

  • Next go to “Connections” and click on “Revoke Access” on the relevant application

Actually, now it’s not a surprise to see big scams on Twitter. These days we hear a lot about Twitter attacks which spread malware, steal passwords or spread scam messages like this one. Now the biggest question is, Can Twitter stop these attacks? Actually, it might be impossible to stop all of them, but at least they can reduce these attacks by increasing their security! For now, Twitter users need to be very carefull! Now, there are two common problems in Twitter. First is the frequent down times and second one is the attacks! So, what do you think? Can you use Twitter with these problems in your head?
