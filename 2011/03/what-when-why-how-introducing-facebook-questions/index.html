What? When? Why? How? : Introducing Facebook Questions?

Mar 26th, 2011 | Comments

There are lots of places where you can ask questions from any stranger. If you ask a question in these places, anyone can come up and give you an answer. But, what if you want to ask a question from your own friends? From people you know well? This is the big problems which Facebook is trying to solve through Facebook Questions!

“There are a lot of places you can go on the Internet to ask questions of people who you don’t know, but there are very few places you can go to get responses from your friends,”

-Adrian Graham

As I said earlier, Facebook is trying to give us an opportunity to ask questions from our own friends in a more professional way. For them, this might be a very easy task because the whole Facebook is already based on friends..

Last year, they started to offer beta versions of Facebook Questions and took a very long time to cook up the final release. This week, they have started to roll out this latest addition to our profiles. If you can’t see it yet, go here and add it to your account.

Now, in the Facebook wall, apart from the normal status, photo, link and video icons, you’ll see a “question” link. To ask a question, you have two options : You can ask a normal question where anyone can add any answer. Or, you can make a poll and give answers by yourself. If you select a poll, still your friends can add custom answers as comments. As your friends vote in the polls, they will automatically fill out to show the percentage. Not only that, it will also show the faces of the people who have just voted.

But the beauty of this feature is, if you make a question, it will be shared with all your friends automatically! So, you just have to sit back and wait for answers.There’s another thing, in a polls, you can add a Facebook fan page as an answer. When you start typing, a drop down list will popup and give you the list of fan pages to add.

As I said earlier, we have a lot of places to ask questions. But, Facebook Questions makes it more personal by allowing us to ask questions only from our friends. For one who have people other than true friends in their profiles, it’s another story. So, what do you think? What will be the first question you want to ask?
