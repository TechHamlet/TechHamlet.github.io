Death Battles of the Web Browsers : Pwn2Own 2011

Mar 10th, 2011 | Comments

The Pwn2Oen contest for hacking browsers is running wild at the moment. The crack down of IE8 and Safari 5 was not that much of a surprise. But wait Chrome and Firefox still on the ring. Pwn2Own contest is a competition for security researchers to bypass the security of web browsers. A successful crack is awarded with an instantaneous 15 000$ bounty prize. The contest is held for three days and tomorrow will be the last day of the competition.  This is the first time Chrome is about to be tested because last time no researcher touched Chrome to crack the security vulnerabilities. Last time Firefox was beaten but this time Firefox capitalized the competition by following a good strategy. More on this below.

Internet Explorer 8 was utterly destroyed by the security researcher Stephen Fewer who used three various techniques crack through the IE 8’s security parts such as ASLR/DEP. He also managed to escape the protected mood of the IE 8 and that was a quite a mesmerizing feat by a independent researcher. Here is what Aarion Portnoy, the organizer of Pwn2Own said about the amazing feat –

“He used three vulnerabilities to bypass ASLR and DEP, but also escape Protected Mode. That’s something we’ve not seen at Pwn2Own before,” Aarion Portnoy

Then next browser that lost the game was the Safari 5 which was hacked by a French Security company Vupen in just 5 SECOND. That was a shocking experience for all Safari users who use it on their Apple products.

From the two candidates who registered to crack Chrome and Firefox one didn’t turn up for the competition. Then the other candidate Team Anon, decides to change their target. Probably going for the Mobile browser  Battle.

The secret of the Chrome and Firefox is visible to all product users because both companies decided to update their browsers with the new version just before the Pwn2Own competition. This strategy worked out great but we are not 100% sure whether the browsers are safe. Security in the new internet sphere has started to get serious because offline criminals are reducing but the online are increasing day by day.  IE and Safari developers will have to learn a lot from these companies because Chrome and Firefox take challenges seriously. More updates are about to arrive and to keep you posted follow my twitter account – Dulitha Wijewantharw
