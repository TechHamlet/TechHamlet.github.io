Climsy to the Rescue : Makes Image Sharing Too Easy!

Mar 25th, 2011 | Comments

Have you ever wanted to share a quick screenshot of your computer with a mate? You might be troubled by the things you have to do! Traditional procedure and the procedure that I use until now is listed below :

1. Press the print screen button, 2. Windows> Start Menu> Paint, 3. Paste the image to Paint, 4. Save File, 5. Upload to ImageShack or Dropbox (I use Dropbox) ,6. Get the link and share the link

Wow… That’s a lot of work you have to do to share a screenshot. But don’t worry. At the end of this post, you will do it with ease and peace. First the program that’s going to help us out here is called as Climsy. It’s a program that runs silently under the hood of windows.

The white duck like icon is the Climsy icon. First you have to download Climsy from their website. Another good thing is that a portable version and an installer is both available. I installed Climsy and then it continues to run under the hood. First you need to configure Climsy. Then you are good to go. Don’t worry Climsy has a small menu. With just 4 items.

Press the setting and you will be take to the settings page. Few things I suggest you to do is to enable Automatically start Climsy After Login option. With out further description adjust your setting to the below screenshot’s settings.

If you don’t won’t your Dropbox account filled with screenshots please disable the automatically upload image option. I found it’s quite frustrating because when an image is cropped in Paint or any other image editing application the image is automatically uploaded. One more thing I have to say. The Climsy icon will turn to orange color from default white when the clipboard contains an image.

Next you press your screenshot button on the keyboard and a small message will be displayed.

Then when you press the Share Image button it will be uploaded to your Imageshack or Dropbox account. Volia! The link to the image too is copied automatically and all you just got to do is to share it.

The pros of this small app is that it reduces the time to take a screenshot and sharing it. It takes a very little windows memory. Another good thing is that we can enable automatic link copy to clipboard function for easy sharing.There were few problems I detected with Climpsy. One is that We have to copy the whole screen. Only option available expect this to press alt+screenshotkeys. This will copy to clipboard the image of the selected application. The cons are that if you want to show a part of your screen you still have to get aid from an image-editing application. Another problem is that you have to use the long link with Climpsy. No URL shortening is still available. I hope that Bit.ly will collaborate with Climpsy and solve this issue. Other than these two issues I couldn’t detect any bad quality about it. Final tip for Climpsy – You can also quit Climpsy if it starts to bother you too much.
