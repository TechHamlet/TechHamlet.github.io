9 Tips to Choose the Best VPS Which Will Go Perfectly With Your Site

Mar 21st, 2011 | Comments

Noticed that we haven’t written anything in the past few days? Actually, the truth is, I was really busy searching for a good VPS. Tried about 6 VPS services, cancelled many of them, wasted some money, wasted some time, spent hours signing peace treaties with support teams and learnt a lot about hosting and VPS servers! Today im going to give you some very important tips which will help you to choose the best server for your site without wasting your time and money!

1) The very first thing you need to keep in your mind is that a VPS is not like a normal shared host. Most of the time, its up to you to configure the whole server! It’s not easy… There are people who offer managed service levels which means they will take care of the server while you take care of your website. But, most of the time, it’s not cheap! Some even charge $100 per month to manage your server. If you are not an expert, do not try to configure a server just by looking at the tutorials… There are also people who gives managed hosting for very low prices. So, if you don’t know about setting up a web server, go for managed hosting. I know how to set up a web server on my personal machine, but that knowledge is not enough in this situation. The most important factors are security and performance.

2) The second most important fact is memory. People put price tags on VPS servers according to the amount of memory they use. So, first of all, contact your host, and learn how much memory your site needs. A heavy site needs a lot of memory and remember, if you are wiling to use Appache as the web server, you’ll need a lot of memory because Appache eats your memory!

3) Next, check the bandwidth and the disk space they are offering. There are some cheap hosts which seems to be offering a good amount of features, but the bandwidth and the storage space they offer might be barely enough to host your site. If you don’t know how much space and bandwidth your site needs, contact your current host and ask them. Get these values before you start searching.

4) Decide what to install on the server… What is the OS? CentOS? Ubuntu?… What is the web server? Appache? Cherokee? Nginx?… These are very critical decisions to make. Remember, select the OS correctly. If you need a server optimized OS, go for CentOS. If you need a user-friendly OS, go for Ubuntu. Remember, you can’t change the OS when the site is running like a lizard change its color. Yesterday I moved Tech Hamlet to a new VPS and got to know the reality about Apache. It took a huge amount of memory making the server to crash. Today in the morning, I found out that the site is down, Appache has crashed! So, if you want a server which will save you some memory, go for Nginx (Light and Fast). Cherokee might be a good decision, for beginners because it have an easy to use web interface to configure everything. But, Nginx have more power. Even Wordpress.com is powered by Nginx. But, there are some hosts which offer server images which already have some software on them. Like, CentOS with LAMP or LEMP, Jumpbox Images, and Turnkey Images. If you are an expert and if you know what you are doing, install everything by your self otherwise, go for a preconfigured server.

5) Check how they offer support. Will they freely install software on your VPS? Will they configure it for you? Will they look at your VPS if something goes wrong? Some hosts doesn’t give any support unless you buy a managed service level. Some only give you partial support and you’ll have to pay a fee every time you make a support request. So, be careful.

6) If you are just testing, If you are willing to cancel the account, if it doesn’t meet your needs, check whether they have money back guarantees. People like 1and1 have minimum service periods before the cancellation and they are very tough. I had to send several e-mails to get an instant refund. They agreed to refund immediately, but still didn’t get it! (The worst host I have tried).

7) If you are not an expert or if you are a reseller, always check whether they offer control panels like Cpanel or Plesk. Be careful, some people charge extra to install these services.

8) Also check whether they offer regular backups. This is very important because you’ll never know when something can go wrong. So, be careful.

9) Always remember that most of these VPS providers will take some time to set up your account. Sometimes they’ll ask for a copy of your ID or they’ll just call you. So, don’t forget to provide a genuine phone number. Also keep in mind, don’t try to get a VPS in a hurry. Don’t try to move at the last-minute (when your server is fully overloaded and your web host have suspended your account) Always keep in mind, they take some time. In the world of Web, every second counts!

Always check these points before you choose your host. Always read their features, policies, FAQs before making your choice. Always go through Google to find reviews. Apart from normal user reviews, always check some professional reviewers like Web Host Gear. When you are dead sure that you need that host, go for it! So, hope these tips will save you from getting into a lot of trouble like me! Trust me, I spent a whole week trying to find a nail in a haystack without even knowing what im looking for and how to look for it. Now if you read the above tips, you know where you are going and you won’t get lost! Now, if you choose a good VPS by following these tips, don’t forget to tell us about your choice with a small comment!
