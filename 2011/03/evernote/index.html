Evernote : Don't Forget It! (Review)

Mar 8th, 2011 | Comments

One of the greatest note taking applications ever built. I personally use it for many things. I organize my hunches, blog posts, and study notes from Evernote . Evernote stores your notes in their servers and you get 60MB for a free account per month. This is great because it’s not that easy to fill the 60mb. Today I am going to discuss in detail the pros and cons of Evernote .

Evernote can become handy to make quick notes and it’s an interesting utility. For us who get hunches quickly forgets them too rapidly. Evernote is the right solution. We can take the notes easily and arrange them notebook wise to organize all ideas easily. Evernote can be used to clip web pages, write notes, write poem, and for many other activities. It has a limitless possibility.

Pros

  • Inline note searching(This feature is one of my most favorites)

  • Desktop app gives offline support. SpringPad doesn’t support offline working.

  • 60MB free space. We can upgrade to Evernote premium  for a cheap price and increase the space to 2GB

  • Can clip web pages directly from many web browser

  • Synchronizing is fast and doesn’t dissolve internet speed

  • Notes can be exported and can be manually protected

  • Great support from platforms

Cons

  • When I copy a post written in Evernote directly to wordpress, it’s kept automatically in DIV tags and this increases the text size. What I do to get around this is to copy to MS Word or notepad and then copy to Wordpress

  • When spelling is corrected the whole word is selected. Well I usually press enter or space when the spelling is corrected and then the word gets lost. So you have to press the next key from the keypad then the space or enter

  • Hard to maintain text styles such as size and font.

These are the only cons I found in Evernote and up to now I am using it. We can hope that Evernote will correct these cons and give us full satisfaction. I highly recommend Evernote to any user because it will increase productivity massively.
