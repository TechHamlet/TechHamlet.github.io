Did You Know:One Quater of Global Population Does Not Use Internet for Health!

Sep 23rd, 2011 | Comments

Hey guys. I’m here to tell you an alarming fact. Did you know that 23% of global Internet user DO NOT use internet for health info? Well this seems to be quite true. Just imagine how many people just hangout in social sites for the sake of being there. They could have actually use that time to do both hanging out and learning some extra health tips. Only a very little people have realised that our body is similar to a car. It needs maintenance, cleaning etc.

This ought to be a terrible situation and the Company who has carried out the survey has estimated that this number would GROW to around 30% in 2016.

Edelman had surveyed more than 16,000 people from all around the world to create a global confidence index with the theme  ‘how healthy different countries believe they are’. While most values seem high, the survey shows how cultural views of health can skew responses.

In India, Armano said, percentage is high because good health is measured just by access to clean water. Japan’s percentage might be low, despite its record of long life-expectancy, due to the recent earthquake, tsunami and ensuing crises. Well for Sri Lanka fortunately, this rate is much higher when compared with the US.

Seems like the developing countries have a higher rate in health than the developed countries. Seems a little not true BUT this are true values. Which rings us the bells that we better change our life styles.

Have they stopped from this? NO. They have taken this survey one step further comparing these values to how people keep up their health and how they use the Internet. The results help illustrate the growing connection between global health and digital revolution.
