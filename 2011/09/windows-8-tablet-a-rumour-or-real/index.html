Windows 8 Tablet- a Rumour or Real?

Sep 21st, 2011 | Comments

Hey fellas well the answer for that is YES it’s real.  Microsoft has kicked off the new Window 8 in tablet form and statistically, it might become more famous than the iPAD 2 (iOS) and the Samsung Galaxy tab.

This Windows 8 is packed with tweaks and many more awesome stuff.

Keypad- One could say that the on-screen keyboard is well laid-out and responsive, and there’s a clever lock-screen system where you choose your own photo -sketch out lines or swipe across it with your finger or the pen and it gives the ability to do whatever you want with it.

Desktop and themes- Like any other windows phone it has custom built desktop and themes to choose for a variety of range.  It have the ability to even customize your desktop.

Apps – Windows 8 tablet also comes with integrated applications such as a real-time weather report system.

Multitasking Ability- The ability of multitasking is a primary feature for a tablet like this. But the way it is integrated with the system is so cool. Actually guys I can’t explain it in my own words so why don’t we take a look at the picture below.

But there are some major drawback in this which you can hardly hear from dealers as Their intention is to sell these off. Just keep these facts in mind. Below mentioned are some of the things that NO ONE will tell you.

Size and weight- Comes with a 11.6 inch display, and users definitely feel it roomy and appealing for reading and viewing content. One of the cons this has is this tablet alone weighs over 2pounds and it’s the heaviest tablet I’ve seen.

Noise- Well it makes a pretty loud noise. You know the noise that makes your laptop when it starts up right? Well this is similar. So for the noise level I would definitely give minus for this tablet.

Heat- Even though the fan is running often and loudly, this get’s super toasty. Sure, it needs to cool that hot Intel Core i5 CPU inside, but the heat, coupled with the fan noise is something most of the people want to hear as the last thing.

Battery life- The battery life on this preview unit was abysmal. Windows’ desktop interface reported about 2.5 to 3 hours of battery life, and it drained down fairly quickly in use. (The Metro style interface shows a battery gauge icon, but it doesn’t let you see the percentage remaining at this time. Presumably, Microsoft will fix this oversight.)

Well in favour I would recommend this tablet to a friend as there are a lot of features in it other than the Noise, Heating up and some other cons. Hopefully when intel produces allow-power chi these cons will be minimum.

And of course, the last three complaints may be solved by the use of ARM chips inside a Windows 8 tablet.
