Future of Communication Technology

Jul 13th, 2011 | Comments

Current technological innovations are looking far more like something out of a science fiction film or novel rather than real life. That’s just the way technology works now. We’re reaching a point where developers are coming up with new approaches and innovations quicker than we can imagine the next step and it all started with a simple phone. It seemed, years ago, that cell phones weren’t that practical, yet now practically everyone seems to own one. It was all born out of a growing desire for communication, but we’ve reached such heights with devices like the HTC 4G Android Phone that it’s hard for people to imagine the next phase when they’re still trying to adjust to current technology. This has led many, both in the industry and in the consumer field, to ask what the future of communication is going to look like.

We’ve come a long way since the days of cave paintings and wax writings. We have diversified our modes of communication to such a point that we’re keeping lines of communication going when we don’t even realize. When we do things like check our Facebook page, pay a bill online, send a Tweet, or simply make a call to someone; we’re communicating and all at blinding speed. This has been made even easier with technology integration. We can interact with our television, computer, and other devices all with our phone. With the evolution taking place, we’re likely to see further integration down the line where everything is completely interconnected and the transfer of data, ideas, and content will be blindingly fast.

Currently, many of our apps and software depend on the storage available on each device’s hard drive. We’re likely to see an increasing movement towards cloud-based services. This won’t just be for mobile devices, but for all the ways we communicate electronically.

Through support hot spots, much of the processing and storage needs for these programs will live in the cloud. This is going to become essential because the programs that developers are creating are becoming increasingly sophisticated. If we didn’t have cloud services and our apps depended solely on available hard drive space, we would be extremely limited to what we could do. With the cloud, applications will be supported through external means, which will free up developers to come up with new ways to use mobile and other various communication devices.

One of the biggest advances will be in the field holographic technology, which is currently being devolved by researchers. We’re not going to see it in the next couple years but it’s very likely that we’ll see it implemented within the next decade. Just as smartphones have changed the face of communication, future holo-tech and cloud services will become the new front in humanities drive to improve the way we reach out to one another. These new innovations will also have far reaching effects and will slowly make their way into every facet of our lives as technology and life slowly merge in to a futuristic amalgamation.
