Droptunes vs Cloud Drive : Battle of the Cloud Music Players

May 16th, 2011 | Comments

Amazon Cloud drive and Droptunes are few of the most awesome way of listening to music online. I have previously written a post about Droptunes so if you want to know a bit more about it it would be the best place to go. Amazon Cloud drive is another awesome service where you can use to store your music and listen to your music online. Possible Google Music is out now too. So let’s start reviewing.

Space

In terms of space Amazon cloud drive gives you 5 GB free space directly. But Dropbox starts basically with 2 GB and you can upgrade it to about 8 GB with loads of hard work. Like getting signups via affiliate links. So for the people who wants to get music online Cloud drive is the place. But with Dropbox it is of a whole different level.

Store

Amazon has a store where you can purchase your music and Amazon player and all the stuff related  to the cloud drive are Amazon products and not third party ones. Droptunes is a plugin app for Dropbox to make music work.

Client

Dropbox has a desktop client that will help your music get on syn with the web easily. Plus it’s really easy. Amazon doesn’t have such a service at the moment. But looking at Google Music it seems that we are going to see an amazing competitor for synchronizing music.

Issues

Amazon cloud drive player has a limitation. It is only for US customers. But with Droptunes anyone can listen to music easily. No problem at all.

Final Decision :

Actually, the best person who can give a good decision is you. So, let us know your own ideas about these two services through comments!
