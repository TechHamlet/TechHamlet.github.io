Get More Affiliate Sales From Your Site by Using These 5 Tips

May 8th, 2011 | Comments

Affiliated marketing can be a great opportunity for a motivated entrepreneur to work from home but it can be extremely challenging to achieve a profitable outcome if one doesn’t know ‘the ropes’. The fact is, affiliate marketing is hard work. A good definition of affiliate marketing would be promoting (on your site) the products and/or services of others in exchange for a commission on completed sales or leads traceable back to your site. If you are considering this form of business or if you are already in the business but without the success you desire, perhaps the five tips listed below will help boost your affiliate sales and contribute to your marketing success!

1. Obtain the Best Possible Conversion Rates

The conversion rate is the percentage of visitors to the site who actually complete the sites intended function. As an example, an affiliate who is promoting weight loss supplements should pay attention to the number of visitors to their site compared to the number who purchase the product. If this number is low, the problem often lies with the site itself.

To get the optimum conversion rate, you should go over your site with a fine tooth comb - that means check out every aspect of the site to ensure it is user friendly. Make sure your site is…

  • Easy for a visitor to navigate

  • There are no broken links

  • Each page has interesting and helpful content

  • The shopping cart is operating properly, and the check-out process is quick and easy

Merely spending the time to evaluate your sites effectiveness and keep it updated can make a remarkable difference in how many visitors actually become valued customers.

2. Grab Visitors Attention then Guide Them to Action

You want to make sure that every visitor to your site is immediately interested in what you have to offer, and then guided quickly to the action you want them to take (buy your product/service). As an example, if you want them to sign on to your mailing list, using ploys like “For your free trial, sign up here” or “Reserve your free copy of…” not only guides the visitor, it entices them to go further by your promise of something free. When encouraging your visitor to buy something, it helps if you tell them of any free services you offer along with the purchase. If you are selling an e-book, remind them that downloads are instant and they don’t have to pay shipping charges and any other benefits they will enjoy; this will make the purchase seem eminently more worthwhile. In this way, you have told them exactly what you want them to do and at the same time made them feel like they are getting a deal.

3. “Just Say No” to Generic Landing Pages

An effective landing page needs to be customized for the visitors you intend to reach. When a PPC visitor lands on your page for a specific purpose, a generic page will not grab their attention and they will be gone as soon as they came. A landing page can’t be a ‘one size fits all’ if you are really serious about making the sale. An example would be if a visitor came to your site looking for advice on how to stop hair loss and your page gave generic advice on caring for beautiful hair, they would find no value in it and be off to another site quickly. Certainly it takes more time and effort to customize your landing page and keep it fresh and interesting, but do take the time required to meet specific needs and you will give your conversion rate a substantial boost.

4. Sound Trustworthy, Look Trustworthy, Be Trustworthy

People are interested in useful services and quality products from people they can trust. So your site should be proudly showing off any awards, celebrity recommendations, or magazine/Television publicity about your product and company. Just by telling your visitor that your product has been recognized in any form of media helps them trust the quality of the product and make them think they have discovered a hot new trend.

5. Privacy Matters

There has been so much media attention given to online security breaches that internet users are understandably nervous about the possibility of identity theft. If a site seems unprofessional in any way, most potential customers will walk away without buying. For this reason, you must reassure visitors that your check-out process is secure and that their personal information will not ever be sold or shared. If the visitor feels comfortable with your security they will be more likely to share their contact information and/or buy your product.

You’ve probably heard the phrase “the devil’s in the details” used in many different ways. It is also true here because while most affiliates know the basics of affiliate marketing, few are familiar enough with the details to make it a truly successful business. So to sum up the information discussed here, take time to win your visitor’s trust, grab their interest and hold it, and meet their individual needs and your reward will be the elevation of your marketing campaign from ‘ordinary’ to ‘financially successful’ within a very short time.
