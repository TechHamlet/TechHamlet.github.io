The Best 5 Project Management Tools

May 14th, 2011 | Comments

The market is flooded with project management software, and it seems like everyone has their favorite.  In truth, most of these programs offer the same basic kit, with solutions for tracking the progress of a project, scheduling milestones, storing and organizing files, and communicating with a team.  Some of them come with discs, some are downloadable, and some are operated entirely online.  There are options available for the self-employed individual all the way up to multi-national corporations that employ thousands of people.  And of course, each one comes with its own unique take on organization. 

1. Basecamp HQ.  This SaaS (software as a service) program is based in online management, so you don’t have to download anything (although you will have to pay a monthly recurring charge of $24 minimum).  However, Basecamp software is a great place to start for any small operation, such as freelance work, a small business, or a small group within a larger business.  It offers tools for task management, data storage, and project communication, along with a well-received support network prepared to offer helpful solutions to any potential problems.  It’s the perfect introduction to project management software.

2.  Sugar CRM.  This software is also SaaS, but it is somewhat more comprehensive than competitors.  For one thing, it offers a lot of added value for the business that is somewhere between the home-based and corporate level.  Slightly more expensive than others (with plans starting at $360/year), it nonetheless offers a streamlined project manager along with tools for marketing, sales, and customer service.  It will not only serve project management needs, it will also help companies to secure more business, increase productivity, and improve customer relations.  That’s a lot of extras for an online project manager.  Oh, and they put their money where their mouth is with a free trial.

3. Taskpoint.  The most unique aspect of this software is its compatibility features.  Although it is entirely adequate as a project management program, it also offers instant connectivity with the ability to link up to a number of mobile devices (so that you can access project information from anywhere) as well as forward-thinking communication tools like an RSS feed and compatibility with Outlook to keep you connected to both employees and clients.  In short, Taskpoint provides for constant interaction and updates, which is great for the business manager on the go.  And with plans as low as $19/month, it’s pretty much perfect for the small-business owner on a budget.

4. Clarizen.  A lot of hype surrounds this recent addition to the project management scene, and it doesn’t seem to be misplaced.  Users across the board find it to be easy and accessible, and there’s no download so you can just jump right in.  Tools for coordination and tracking are relatively simple (with solutions for budgeting, prioritizing, status updates, and so on), but it is the considerations for delegation and oversight that set it apart from competitors.  Plans are competitively priced, starting at $24.95/month.

5. Microsoft Project.  If you run a small business, you might not have much use for this software, even though it advertises to all levels of project management.  In truth, the $900 price tag will make it less appealing to businesses on a budget, as will the many tools that are geared towards unified coordination of multiple projects and users.  Although it is, of course, compatible with other Microsoft products, which makes it appealing to a wide audience, it’s really better suited to large operations.
