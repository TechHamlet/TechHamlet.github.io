Twitter Buys TweetDeck for $40 Million

May 24th, 2011 | Comments

Before sometime there was a big rumor going on about the purchase of TweetDeck and the fight to acquire it between UberMedia and official Twitter. TweetDeck holds the monopoly of Tweets coming from apps and it has become a Tweeter Control panel for many people. Even CNNMoney has confirmed the deal.

Now for a little history about TweetDeck. TweetDeck company was started by Iain Dodsworth in 2008. It is situated in London, Britain and it has investors like Accelerator Group and Betaworks backing them up. Now TweetDeck has mad apps for Desktop, iPhone, iPad, Android, Chrome. Now they are working on a Web interface too and it’s on the beta level. It will be supported by browsers like Safari, Firefox, and Internet Explorer.

UberMedia tried really hard to get their hands on TweetDeck but it seems that they have failed and official Twitter has taken over the deal. Twitter was making remarks to developers saying not to develop apps that mimics Twitter website because Twitter wanted to maintain the monopoly of tweets. Around 40% of Tweets come from Apps and it’s generally easier to use twitter with a third party app. So Twitter would have bet everything to get there hands on TweetDeck from UberMedia.

But even for now Twitter hasn’t yet made an official statement whether they have acquired TweetDeck but we can tell that this is one of the greatest triumphs of the Twitter company. What can we expect from this new collaboration. Twitter might decide to allow the simplicity approach from the twitter web site and allow kick-butt interaction via the TweetDeck app set and the new web interface beta. We eagerly waiting for Twitter’s official comments about the matter.
