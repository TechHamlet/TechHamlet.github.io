How to Switch Back to the Old Gnome Interface in Ubuntu 11.04

May 3rd, 2011 | Comments

Recently, Ubuntu released the final version of Ubuntu 11.04 which have changed a lot! The biggest change we saw is the addition of Unity as the default desktop. Unity is an alternative to the Gnome Shell built by the Ubuntu Team. At first, they created it for the Netbook Edition. Many people liked to use it on their Netbooks. But now, its coming as the default interface even for the Desktop version!As I said earlier, many people liked to use Unity in the Netbook Edition. But, things didn’t work out the same way for the desktop edition. Ever since Ubuntu announced about their plans for Natty, many people started to appreciate it, and just like that, many people also started to criticize it. At first, it looked like it’s too unstable. But the final version have fixed most of those bugs. Still, many people find it difficult to adapt to this environment. Unity is designed to make things easier. But for me, the old ways are easier. (That’s my opinion. It depends on the person which use Unity. So, think before you criticize my words!).

Before the big launch, we held a small poll to see what our readers think about Unity. Nearly 64% of our readers didn’t like Unity. They wanted the old interface. If you already know much about Ubuntu, you might have figured how to switch back. But, for new comers, here’s how to do it :

  • When you boot your machine, Ubuntu asks for your password (as shown in the below screenshot)

  • In here, select your user name. After selecting, you will see some icons and menus on the bottom of the screen

  • In there, select the “Ubuntu” drop down menu and select “Ubuntu Classic” from the list (as shown in he screenshot)

  • Now, enter your password and login

If you have followed the instructions, you must see the old Gnome Interface. Now, every time you login, “Ubuntu Classic” will be selected by default untill you switch it back!
