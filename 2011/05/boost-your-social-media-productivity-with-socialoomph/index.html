Boost Your Social Media Productivity With SocialOomph

May 1st, 2011 | Comments

SocialOomph being a Canadian company was formerly known as TweetLater.com with a focus on productivity solutions for users of Twitter.com, and later they broadened their scope to the users of other social networking services and they change the name to “SocialOomph.” Now it’s a tool to boost your “social media” productivity for Twitter, Facebook, StatusNet and your blog/s too. You can use their services to schedule tweets & StatusNet updates (on the FREE account) and Facebook statues & blog posts (on the PAID account)

FREE features for Twitter

  • You can schedule tweets; publish tweets when your international followers are online and you’re asleep

  • Set up alerts and track keywords from the public timeline, so you can view those tweets later

  • Save tweets and re-use those drafts as templates; avoid typing tweets like: “Good morning everyone, have a nice day!” everyday

  • Add and manage as many twitter accounts as you want

  • Private Tweet Viewing  – view selected tweets from breaking news sites, technology news sites, musicians, etc. (for easy viewing of tweets that relate to a specific topic) and much more…

  • Send DMs to your new followers thanking them for choosing to follow you (for a low-cost)

PROFESSIONAL features for Facebook

  • Schedule status updates for future times/dates. You can keep your status updates ticking even when you don’t have access to the internet

  • Schedule posts to the walls of any Facebook Page of which you are an administrator

PROFESSIONAL features for your blog/s

  • Integrate your blog into SocialOomph and schedule posts

  • Manage unlimited blogs under one SocialOomph account and schedule posts for all of them

  • Write posts with the WYSIWYG editor so you can create and format blog posts exactly the way you want

And there’s so much more you can do with SocialOomph (Professional) Note: you get a 7-Day FREE TRIAL of SocialOomph Professional

I recommend you to slightly go around their “Terms and Conditions” and  “Privacy Policy” before using their services; it’s for your good, you’ll feel more comfortable.

Socialoomph provides their API for developers and you can read their blog at SocialoomphBlog.com
