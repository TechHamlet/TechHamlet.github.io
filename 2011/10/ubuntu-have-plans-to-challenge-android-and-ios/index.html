Ubuntu Have Plans to Challenge Android and iOS!

Oct 31st, 2011 | Comments

Most of you already know that Ubuntu is the most popular Linux distribution on the planet. In the past few years, they started to show some love towards mobile devices. They started with net-books by introducing a special version for net-books with a special UI called Unity. Now, they have taken it to their desktop edition, and now working hard to improve it. Now, Unity is not just a new desktop… It’s a new desktop aimed for a new era of Ubuntu!

Recently, the father of Ubuntu Mark Shuttleworth had an interview with ZDNet. On this interview, he have mentioned that they are planning to make Ubuntu available for mobile devices like smart phones, tablets and even smart TVs.

“This is a natural expansion of our idea as Ubuntu as Linux for human beings. As people have moved from desktop to new form factors for computing, it’s important for us to reach out to out community on these platforms. So, we’ll embrace the challenge of how to use Ubuntu on smartphones, tablets and smart-screens.”

As a Ubuntu user I think this is going to be great. A free open source OS to be used on all the devices you use! But already we have a free open source Linux based mobile OS on our devices. Already Android have received a lot of popularity and there’s also iOS to deal with. But, Mark thinks that they can do it. Don’t think they have just taken this decision right now. According to Mark, they have been talking about this program with their partners for 18 months and Unity is the first step!

On the next Ubuntu release (12.04) they are going to make Unity more stable and reliable. But, it looks like you won’t be able to see this new mobile versions until the year 2014! They need time to plan this. Also, they want to release it with a LTS which means things are more stable than the other versions. So, don’t start counting your fingers now.

Not only Ubuntu, even Windows have stated to think about mobile devices. The new Windows 8 is going be released for all these platforms. Like Unity in Ubuntu, Windows have  the Metro UI.

You know what I think? I think introducing mobile versions is great. But, taking the mobile UI to the desktop is not a good idea. Unity is a good example for this. People liked Unity when they introduce it in the net-book edition. But, when Ubuntu introduced it to the Desktop edition, they started to lose their fans. I think the same problem will happen to Windows 8 also. What do you think?
