Introducing Tech Hamlet 12.0 : A New Design, HTML 5, a New Host and Many More...

Oct 7th, 2011 | Comments

Our site is getting bigger… The web is getting newer… The technology is changing… So, we need to keep up with this changing world! That’s why we always update our site… Say hello to the 12th version of TechHamlet.com. A better design, a new host, new technology, more reliable, more stable and more new excitements!

Earlier, we decided what to do with the site and we put our own ideas… Fortunately, most of you liked them. But this time, we did a small change. At first, we asked you what you need and you gave us a very good feedback. Now, here’s our first design which we created based on your own ideas…

Since December 2010, we started using Thesis and tried to put our own custom design. So, the version 10.0 is mostly unique to TH. But, we really got some ideas from our previous design. So, we can say that its half custom! But this time, our site is 100% unique. The whole site is redesigned (Again with the help of Thesis).

The Latest Web Technologies…

These days the whole web is changing due to the latest web standards HTML5 and CSS3. They have brought new life to the web. For several months people were hesitant to move to these technologies because the old browsers didn’t support them. But, to answer this problem, many browsers  have accelerated their release cycles so that they can keep up with the fastly growing web.

I think its now time for us to go into the world of HTML5 and CSS3! Currently, Thesis doesn’t support HTML5. But, we did some hacks and turned some parts of the framework into HTML5 markup. Also we used heavy CSS3 tricks like CSS3 animations. Most of the moving stuff you now see in our site is not JavaScript (Except for the Category menu) ! They are pure CSS!

Our code is now W3C Valid…

That’s not all… Earlier, we never cared about the validity of our code.. We didn’t knew how much it is important. This time, we tried our best to keep the code clean and 100% valid. Actually, we were unable to achieve this completely. We managed to make our home page W3C Valid, but, the rest of the site shows several small issues. This is because of the plugins we used. Our code is valid, but many plugin developers won’t care about this. Even the biggest plugins like Disqus give several errors! So, what shall we do? We can’t build a blog without plugins! I think its ok because many big sites are not 100% valid. For example, Lifehacker have 68 errors, and Mashable have 208 errors! We don’t have any errors in the home page and have 4 to 19 errors showing up in our other pages. We are good.

Simple and more elegant…

You asked for a simple design but there were also people who voted for fascines. So, we combined both of them. Our new design have stripped off many widgets from the sidebar making it more simple. Have also stripped off some other tiny things so that you can focus on the articles. I think now the text is more clearer than ever! Bigger, styled well, better fonts provided by Google… So, what do you think?

A more powerful server…

Earlier, we tested several hosts and stayed with Servint for a long time. They are a really good host but we need more freedom to customize our servers. But, Servint have Cpanel VPS hosting. So, we came into Media Temple! I think you already know that Media Temple is one of the best servers in the world. They are very popular for their quality. So, we decided to give it a shot. Now I think things are more faster because we optimized and customized our server specially for Tech Hamlet. I think it’s a very good choice.

Not only that, we have a lot more core improvements to our site and have also some big future plans. Tech Hamlet is getting stable, so, we are ready to dive deeper and start more projects. What do you think? What should we do next? Actually I like to here new ideas from our own readers… So, let them flow in… Also, don’t forget to leave a comment about our new changes…! Enjoy the new TH!
