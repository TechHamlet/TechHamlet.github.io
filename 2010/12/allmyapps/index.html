A Brand New App Store for Windows : AllMyApps

Dec 24th, 2010 | Comments

If you have been using an iPhone you should probably be using the Apple iPhone App Store, or if you have been using an Android phone you would be using the Android Market to download tons of applications for your phone. Well, it’s always pleasant to have such kind of App Store for Windows too. Nothing to worry now. Here we unveil a decent app store for Windows too; AllMyApps is what we wanted for a long time.

Just as any other app store would do, AllMyApps lets you to search, get detailed descriptions of software, and download in just a single click! Nevertheless, you still have to bear in mind that its in beta stage and as fresh as it is!

In order to get started, you have to sign-up for an account (free!) and download the app store desktop client. Or if you have already installed it on your PC you can simply create a list and install them just from their website. It has a similar interface like the Ninite installer’s website that we mentioned in our previous post. You’ll get a confirmation link to your inbox and upon completion you can download the application.

AllMyApps app store looks handy and has a interface which resembles iTunes’ interface. You need to enter your login details of the account you created in their website in order to use it. On the left pane of the home screen comprises of the software categories such as Web, Communication, Security, Utilities, Office, Video, Audio, Imaging, Games and Development.

When you click on a certain application you can view a detailed description and install right from there. What’s more in it? Well, you can any number of app lists and share them using Twitter or Facebook with your friends. For now, AllMyApps comes with only free apps at the time of writing and we hope they’ll add paid applications in near future.

Verdict

What we like : first of its kind, packs up many free apps, nice and easy-to-use interface.

What we don’t like : Some apps aren’t updated and they could have added many sharing options.

Oh yeah, we can’t forget the fact that still it’s in beta stage and if you encounter with any bugs just report them.

AllMyApps marks the beginning of a whole new trend of Windows applications and stay tuned for the stable release!
