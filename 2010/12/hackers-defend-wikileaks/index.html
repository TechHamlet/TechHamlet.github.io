Hackers Defend Wikileaks by Taking Down Many Websites!

Dec 9th, 2010 | Comments

There are many forces coming ahead to defend Wikileaks by attacking various other sites that are “bowing towards government’s pressure” . A group of hackers of online imageboard site, 4chan have already taken their steps to get down site such as Paypal, Mastercard, PostFinance and Visa.com

Hackers have conducted DoS(Denial of Service) attacks as soon as Paypal ceased Wikileaks’ creator Julian Assange’s Paypal account and other accounts which are raising funds for the sake of Wikileaks. The attack has started from The Swiss bank’s PostFinance site and it’s still offline as I was writing this article.

Hackers, namely “Anonymous” have also attacked the Paypal site and they were only able to get down Paypal’s blog. The Tug of War still remains. Now small unknown groups of hackers are trying to attack Anonymous‘ own site.

WikiLeaks’ main source still remains unknown. As the name suggests there must be some sort of “leak” in the government sector. EveryDNS, The DNS provider of WikiLeaks has also decided to cut down all the connections with it. Hackers who defend WikiLeaks has already attacked Visa and Mastercard’s site too. But those sites were restored back again.

Moreover, hundred of mirror sites of WikiLeaks has also appeared online and an encrypted torrent file of the cable dump of WikiLeaks is also can be found on the internet. They claim that if this war still remains unchanged, the decryption key will be provided globally.

We still don’t know how far this would go. But it’s going to be more and more interesting in the future as far as this matter is concerned.
