Get More Blog Readers Using Yahoo Answers

Apr 5th, 2010 | Comments

Today is the time of Yahoo Answers and other such question answer services. But Yahoo Answers being the most popular, everyone goes in there. And its quality too! In Yahoo Answers all the posts there are of great quality unlike in some other places. Most of the people including me searching for answers on their questions after searching on Google and not finding a suitable answer turn to Yahoo Answers to get their questions answered and it really helps them. I visit Yahoo Answers and love answering the questions asked by the users there.

Why Answer The User Questions?

The answer to this question is very simple. You want more readers, answer more questions. When you answer any question do not forget to add your blog link in the source field. Otherwise there is no use of answering as the asker would not know who and from where answered the question. He also feels that if he visits that site or link he will get more information and he visit the site. If he likes the articles there, he is now a regular reader of that site. Wasn’t it simple, getting one reader by answering one question?

Every time The Asker Won’t Visit Your Link!

The above point says that answering questions with your link will make the asker visit your site in search for more information. But in many cases this doesn’t appear to be true. Many people get satisfied with the original answer you’ve given and don’t want any more information. Though there are very less people with such thoughts, there are some who think this way. So add a line “For More Information, Visit The Link Given Below Or In The Source Field”. This won’t do wonders, but certainly you will get some users more into your site.

The Links Appear To Be No-Follow, Are They Really?

Well the answer to this question is “YES”. The links in the source field as well as anywhere in the answer are completely no-follow. I think Yahoo guys use this as a precautionary measure to avoid giving links to harmful sites which can reduce their ranks. Also as there are innumerable questions on Yahoo Answers and each question with many answers it isn’t good making links Do-Follow. If we think as an expert, I support this policy. But as a part of a blogger, I don’t like this at all.

Answering Questions Is Too Time Consuming!

Yes it might be a little time consuming, but you build a reputation there and also you do get some readers and benefit from them. The point that answering is time consuming is true, but it gives you a lot of benefits too. A lot of users and answerers time could have been saved if Yahoo Answers had an answer preview column in the same page where we typed the answer. It currently isn’t so. First user has to click on answer link, then type the answer, preview, submit, etc. This is a very long process. Instead they could have added a quick reply sort of feature where in the answerer could answer the question right below the question without going in the various pages. This gives Yahoo Answers a negative point. Hope they include this feature soon.

Will There Be A Sudden Boost Of Readers?

No, this isn’t any magic trick friends. You might benefit from this in the long run. There might not be an immediate boost of visitors, readers and page views, but slowly and steadily you will progress. But even if you get some readers from there, you won’t be able to know did they come from Yahoo Answers as they didn’t come by clicking a link on the site. They copied the link manually and came in your site. So you won’t be able to find this even if you use super-powerful Statistics tools!
