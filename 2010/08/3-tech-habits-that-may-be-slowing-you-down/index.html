3 Tech Habits That May Be Slowing You Down

Aug 18th, 2010 | Comments

We all love excuses and we love using them the most to avoid doing things to help ourselves. This is no different when it comes to getting that new start-up out the door, completing that project for class , or even doing something as small as finally getting out there to clean the car.

If you are anything like me, a lover of all tech, you try to avoid anything that doesn’t involve something that needs bytes to run. Although sometimes you have to wonder is our beloved technology actually slowing us down. Here are a few tech habits that I have found slow even the most focused, productive person down.

1. Browsing the Internet.

I don’t care what I am trying to procrastinate on, my number one culprit always seems to be that darned Internet. I could try to blame it on the wealth of information I’m learning from all the “how-to save time” articles, but the truth is I fall victim to checking my email knowing nothing is there but monthly newsletters and Facebook alerts. I also waste tons of time browsing through Google Reader which was essentially setup to help me, but only helps me slip into hours of blog browsing.

So what is a girl supposed to do, besides close the tabs or never open them although somehow through my procrastination I find a way to get back to my browsing my favorite blogs. TURN OFF THE INTERNET! It’s an unfortunate thing I know, I mean a few hours a day without the Internet, what are you supposed to do – well the answer is work on getting something done.

2. Using Digital To-Do Lists.

This is coming from the queen of to-do lists but I honestly get less done when I use them on my phone or online. I use great apps to list everything I must do and wake up the next day to work down my list just to realize I’ve ignored it or haven’t reached my goal by midday.

Realistically you can only get so many things done in one day and that project does not get done by making tons of to-do lists, it gets done by you actually working on the things on the to-do list. So either hand write (GASP!) or mentally keep a short list of 5 things you MUST get done that day and you may believe it or not get done much quicker.

3. Using your phone – for everything.

Fortunately and unfortunately, our phones can do everything these days and this is the problem. Our phones are our inboxes, our access to the world through the Internet, our radios (with Pandora), our game consoles, basically our EVERYTHING. You need time to get away from everything sometimes and this is the thing that periodically just needs to be turned off.

Take some time just one day, to log how much time you actually spend on your phone, then note how much of that time you spend doing nothing even seemingly productive. You will quickly realize our best, productive friends also known as our phones can sometimes be just another habit we need to kick so we can truly be productive.

I’m not saying these exact habits are the DEATH habits for everyone, but I find reading online and talking with other the same rings true. You don’t have to give up your tech to be productive, this is far from the point, but the balance of technology and good ‘ole well tech breaks can get the job done sometimes.

What do you think? What piece of technology or bad tech habit do you find helps you waste the most time? Let us know.
