How to Remote Control Your PC With a Tweet!

Aug 8th, 2010 | Comments

Today you can Tweet from your mobile phone, from your laptop, from your friend’s computer or even from your workplace! But, can you control your PC from anywhere you want? The harsh truth is, you need to be in front of your machine to tell it what to do! Or is it right? Not any more.. now prepare yourselves to control your PC from anyplace where you can write a single Tweet!

People use remote controls to control a device while staying away from that device. Normally, every Television set will come with a remote control. But your PC will never come with such a remote control. But now you can build your own remote control with a little free software called Tweet My PC.

Simply, this software will scan your twitter account for specific commands and as soon as you tweet such a command, the software will execute the related task on your system! For example, if you tweet “shutdown” then Tweet My PC will shutdown your computer! Not only that, you can define custom commands to run your own software when you tweet. So, want to try it? Lets see how..

  • First download the software from this link : Tweet My PC

  • Next, open Tweet My PC and enter your Twitter account’s login details as shown below” :

  • If you want to add your own custom commands, you can do it by going into Settings >  Custom Commands

  • After your done click on “Save and Hide”

Now you can send a tweet and control your machine! A complete list of commands can be found  here : http://tweetmypc.codeplex.com/wikipage?title=Commands. All you got to do is just tweet the command you want and your PC will surely obey you. So, what do you think? Isn’t this what you needed when you go away from your machine? When you go outside to visit your friend?
