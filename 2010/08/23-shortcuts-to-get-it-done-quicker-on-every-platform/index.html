23 Shortcuts to Get It Done Quicker on Every Platform

Aug 16th, 2010 | Comments

We all use technology to help us get things done faster and efficiently, but sometimes this effort goes out the window when we ourselves hinder the device’s efficiency by doing things the hard way.

I have compiled a quick reference sheet of shortcuts you can use on some of your favorite devices, operating systems, and browsers that will help you get the job done quicker. Some of them you may know, love, and recognize, some of them you may have never seen before- but these shortcuts definitely need to be in your well stocked shortcut repertoire.

Windows

  1. Quick domains. Press Ctrl + enter to quickly adds the [www.] and the [.com] to an address.

  2. Find a File QUICKLY! Press the F3 key to find your files quickly.

  3. Jet to the Desktop. Get to the desktop fast from anywhere by pressing Windows Logo+D.

  4. Don’t play just yet! Press SHIFT when inserting a CD-ROM, stops from automatically running.

Mac

  1. Shut it down, NOW. Use Cmd+Ctrl+Opt+Eject to shut your Mac down instantly (again with no dialog box or warning).

  2. Expose. Press F9 anytime you want to see all the windows you have open, trust me, it’s much quicker.

  3. Find your Desktop. Press F11 and your desktop will magically appear (not really magically, but you get it).

Linux

  1. Clear the terminal. Press Ctrl + L to clear everything in the terminal.

  2. Bring it back. Bring your processes sent to the background to the main stage with Command+fg.

Internet Explorer

  1. Print Fast. Need to print in a hurry, just press CTRL+P to get the job done.

  2. Quicker Searches. No need waste time visiting your favorite search engine to find the answer, just press CTRL+E to find what you need automatically.

Safari

  1. Quick Domain. Domain Name (i.e. SimpleLeap) + Shift + Return gets you to your web destination quicker.

  2. Go back, hurry! The delete key takes you back a page without leaving the keyboard.

  3. Quick Google Search. Press Cmd+Opt+F to quickly perform a Google search.

FireFox

  1. New Tab What’s even quicker than “Ctrl+T” is simply clicking the link with your Mouse Scroll Button (is Using Linux or Windows). This instanly opens the link in a new tab.

16.  Text Size Increase If you find yourself squinting press Ctrl + “+” to increase the text size of the page.

iPhone

  1. Space bar for Sentences. Don’t worry about typing the period at the end of you sentences, simply double tap the space bar and it will do the work for you.

  2. Quick caplocks. Simply hold down shift (the “UP” arrow on the left of the keyboard) as you type.

  3. Straight to voicemail. Press the power button, the small silver button located at the top of your iPhone, twice and the call will be immediately sent straight to voicemail!

Android

  1. Rush to the bottom. Get to the bottom of the page fast with Spacebar in any web page or document.

  2. Get rid of that line. Noticed an entire line of text is wrong, don’t fret just press Alt + Del to get rid of that line.

BlackBerry

  1. Move back and forth between emails. Press “P” to read the previous email or “N” to read the next email

  2. Scanning your emails. Press the “Space bar” to move down one screen and the “Shift + Space bar” to move up one screen.

What are some of your favorite shortcuts?
