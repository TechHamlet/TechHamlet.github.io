5 Ways to Avoid Becoming "the Tech Guy"

Aug 15th, 2010 | Comments

I always find myself stuck with the guy in the corner of the room at parties that has no clue what ‘this iPod hoopla’ is all about and wants to totally pick my brain of any knowledge I’ve attained around this subject. It gets even better when every visit to the parent’s home turns into the annoying task of virus scans and spyware removal, just to receive the unwanted referrals to all the family friends to perform this exciting task for them as well for FREE.

Well I have some solid tricks that I’ve picked up over time that have officially tarnished my reputation as the ‘tech person’ and in no time, you can lose your reign as family and friend tech go-to as well.

1. Beat Them at their Own Game.

I say if you can’t beat them, join them. Yes, this is cliche but the truth is to beat that dutiful rap as the go-to tech guru you must hit them with your best shot. This means if they are asking for your help with this Twitter site they’ve been hearing about, ask them “Is that the site with the blue duck at the top?”. How about someone asking for help with “The Facebook” (as my mom refers to it)? Well simply counter them with a reference to Facebook as as ‘that silly place all the kid’s share those dumb photos.’ Trust me,  after saying that they will go into all the bad things they’ve heard on the news about Facebook and will absolutely forget they wanted your help – brilliance!

2. Always have Geek Squad’s card in hand.

I swear to you, the next time you visit Best Buy become close friends with your favorite Geek Squad rep and get a card, lots of them, you will need them (especially for those family-friend soirees). Never run out, because when you get hit with the “Hey…I haven’t seen you in a while, your mom told me you know computers and I’m having trouble with…” line you can literally stop them mid sentence and tell them you know a better guy for the job, his name is “Joe” and he’s a genius at fixing that exact issue, I have his card right here. See how that worked, you are nipped as the ‘tech guru go-to’, before it has a chance to go any further.

3. Don’t Tell Your Mom.

Ok, although I’m not encouraging you to lie to your mother because this would be wrong…not telling her about your particular tech interests in your life will spare you a little agony. Always be broad and avoid the “C” or “T” words (computer and technology – respectively) in any of your conversations. Let’s say you are a Whitehat hacker for a local tech company, your mom should simply know that you monitor some sort of in-office security system with a team of colleagues. Do you develop apps for a small software firm? No problem, tell your mom you are one of the lead production leaders on a team dedicated to making the firm more productive. Don’t look at these as lies, look at these as the exact phrases that will spare you the uncomfortable task of saying no thanks to all of those wonderful family-friend data retrieval volunteer job offers.

4. Never be the ‘right person’.

So somehow they found out that you ‘know computers’ (goodness I hate that phrase – anyway). Don’t panic, you still have options. If they are looking for someone to make their computer ‘run faster’ then sadly , yes put on your best sad face act – you only do computer graphics. Maybe they need someone that makes websites , perfect you only know code that makes calculators run and for good measure rant about how worthless that calculator coding skill really is. Get creative with it!

5. Give them a high rate.

If those around you simply ‘insist’ that you are the one they would trust and love to “fix their computer” and all else fails – quote that HUGE rate. That’s right, if you don’t want to do it for them, go out of your way to quote the most ridiculous rate for removing those silly games they always install that of course installs spyware and watch their eyes just fly out of their sockets. It will forever be in their mind that you are just ‘too high’ and you are miraculously off their ‘tech guy’ list. It’s that simple – because who are we kidding your genius is never enough to pay for to them. The high rate gets the job done EVERY TIME!

Look, my article is not saying to not share your tech knowledge every blue moon with a loved one or good friend, but honestly we all know having the ‘tech guru’ title is often times burdensome and these are great, funny ways to get out of it.

What do you use to get out of your possible tech encounters?
