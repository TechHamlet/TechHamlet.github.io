Firefox 3.6.6 Have Been Released to Fix a Major Issue in the Last Release!

Jun 27th, 2010 | Comments

On June 22, the Mozilla Foundation announced the release of Firefox 3.6.4 which is said to be adding more stability to your browser. One of the main reasons a browser can crash is because of the plugins (specially flash) crash, bringing down the whole browser! To solve this problem, Firefox thought of moving the plugins out of the main process just like Google Chrome which will make the crash to only affect the current page.. But after the release, this new feature turned out to have one major problem!

If one of your browser plugins hangs for 10 seconds without any response, the new crash protection feature will terminate the plugin to save the browser. But, most of the users who use old computers had problems especially with online games.

Sometimes, the game will not respond for more than 10 seconds, but its not crashed… its still running… At this stage, Firefox will think that the plugin have crashed and it will terminate the plugin to avoid further damage.

After getting complaints from several users, Firefox decided to fix this issue immediately and release Firefox 3.6.6. Unlike 3.6.4, the new version will wait for 45 seconds for a plugin to respond before killing it. This new release should fix the issue and give you a smooth browsing! So, update your browser right now!

How to update :

  • Open your Firefox browser. In the menu, go to “Help” > “Check for Updates” or “Apply DownloadedUpdates now”

  • Or download it from here : http://www.mozilla.com/en-US/firefox/personal.html
