How to Bring the Office Web Apps to Your Desktop

Jun 15th, 2010 | Comments

These days Microsoft is very busy preparing the next major version of their famous Office suit. This new Office 2010 is said to be having more new features which will allow you to work with the web. But, as usual, this suit comes with a big price tag! So, many people wanted to use the Free Google Docs provided by Google as an online alternative to Office. But, recently, Microsoft wanted to draw some attention from Google Docs, so they introduced Office Web Apps which is a free online version of their famous Office suit!

Most of the people will love to use this free version instead of spending a lot of money to buy the whole software. Of-course the normal Office Suit have more features than the free web based version. But the Office Web Apps also have some great advantages.. The main advantage is that it is offered Free of Charge! The second is.. Office Web Apps comes with Microsoft’s Sky Drive which is a free online storage service. So, with Office Web Apps, you’ll get a 25 GB free space online, to store all your documents which is much more reliable (and sometimes more safer) than your own Hard Drive!

Normally, to use Google Docs and Office Web Apps, you need to go to your browser! Actually, some people won’t like this. So, we found a small solution for this problem by bringing Office Web Apps to your desktop. Actually, what we are going to do here is, add a shortcut icon in your desktop to Office Web Apps which will open a completely independent browser window making you feel much better! So… What are we waiting for.. lets start our work :

Ingredients (What you should have) :

  • You’ll need Google Chrome Browser which can be downloaded from here : http://www.google.com/chrome

Method (How to do the trick) :

  • First, Open Google Chrome and got to Office Web Apps (http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/web-apps/redir/XT101857758.aspx)

  • After that, click on “Page Menu” in the top right corner as shown in the image below :

  • From that page menu, select “Create Application Shortcuts”

  • Now select where you want to place your shortcuts (as shown below) and click “Create”

  • After your done, Chrome will take that page out from the browser to a different window.

No Address bars, No tabs, No Bookmark bars… very similar to your normal Office Software! So, what do you think? Is it better or not? Please feel free to share your ideas with us!
