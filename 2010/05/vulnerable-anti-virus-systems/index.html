Most of Our Anti Virus Systems Are Vulnerable?

May 10th, 2010 | Comments

We have a huge amount of Anti Virus software in the market to protect us against the viruses that haunts the computers. But, can they really protect us? Can they give us a complete security without any holes? Most of you might know that the simple answer is “No”. And today, we got proof to prove this theory!

Recently, a company called Matousec have taken some of the most famous anti virus systems and tested them by trying to attack them. It seems to be, 100% of the software that they have tested were vulnerable to their attacks! Even the big names like Kaspersky, BitDefender, McAfee, Norton and ZoneAlarm are among them.

If a product uses SSDT hooks or other kind of kernel mode hooks on similar level to implement security features it is vulnerable. In other words, 100 % of the tested products were found vulnerable.

To see whether your Anti Virus is also listed, see this report : KHOBE – 8.0 earthquake for Windows desktop security software

This proves that we are not completely protected from malware at all! So, we need to be more careful when dealing with these pests. An anti virus alone can’t protect us. We should make sure that we run the necessary protection, we should make sure that we stay away from known threats on the Internet, we should make sure that we scan every external device that we insert to our system…. There are lots of thing that you can do to protect yourself like this .

So, my advice is, never leave your anti virus to deal with the malware by its own! Make sure you do everything from your side. In this way.. we might be able to reduce chance of getting infected. But still, we need to remember one thing… the World Is Not Completely Safe! Bu the Internet Is Not Safe At All!
