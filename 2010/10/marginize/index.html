See What Others Are Saying and Share Your Own Thoughts!

Oct 18th, 2010 | Comments

Some web sites have shown what others are saying about them through commenting systems and various social network badges.. But, to get the exact ideas of other people, you need to click several links and spend some time.. Some people doesn’t show what people are saying about them. The big question is, how can I see what people have really said about the site that I have just visited?

Marginize is a small tool which will answer this big question! As the name implies, its a small margin (a sidebar) which will show you what the world is saying about a particular site.

You can use this tool to for two main things :

  • To learn about the true nature of a web site. Whether it have a bad user experience.. Whether its trustworthy..

  • To have fun and meet great web explorers like you and share your ideas with them!

It works like this.. If you visit a website and open Marginize, it will check Twitter, Facebook and Google Buzz and show you what people have said about that site in those three places. Well, currently they support only those three services, but in the future it might grow!

Don’t worry.. They won’t let you stay with your mouth shut! They will also allow you to jump into the conversation and share your own ideas with the others in those three places. It also have a small “Check-in” feature like in other location based social networks.

This simple tool comes as a small browser extension which you can download from their home page (Marginize). Now what happens if some one who doesn’t have this tool comes to a site that you have shared? They have also thought about this problem. If your friend follows the link you shared from Marginize, then he will see the Marginize sidebar as an HTML version which is only valid for that page!

Very useful isn’t it? Now you can be a web explorer or a famous web critic by using this simple tool! So, what do you think? For what will you use this tool? Please feel free to share your ideas with us!
