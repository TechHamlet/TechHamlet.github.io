Easy EBook Management With Calibre

Nov 28th, 2010 | Comments

Always worried about when you see your ebooks sitting here and there on your PC? Let’s put a full stop to it today. Calibre is a multi-platform, free and open-source ebook library management app that would save you a tons of time. It does not only manage your ebook collection, but also it has some cool features like fetching news from over 225 news sources,converting them to various formats and allows you to sync your collection remotely!

Installing Calibre

As it is a cross platform application all the users can fetch the respective installer from the download section of Calibre’s site.

Windows users : http://calibre-ebook.com/download_windows

Mac Users : http://calibre-ebook.com/download_osx

Linux Users : http://calibre-ebook.com/download_linux

For linux, either you can just download and install the from the source. But it would be more easier to get it off from the repos.

Ubuntu users just open up a terminal and type :

sudo apt-get update && sudo apt-get install calibre

Easy enough!

Using Calibre

When you run for the first time you’ll be presented with a welcome screen and there you’ll be asked to what kind of portable ebook reader you use. If you don’t use any portable ebook reader and just view them on your PC just select “Default”.

The main interface is well organized and you’ll be amazed to see what it offers. Here’s a quick at its basic features.

  • Library Management

  • E-book conversion

  • Syncing to e-book reader devices

  • Downloading news from the web and converting it into e-book form

  • Comprehensive e-book viewer

  • Content server for online access to your book collection

Adding books is a piece of cake. It allows you to add ebooks from your hard drive, or from ISBN in which it will automatically download metadata and covers for you!

Once you have successfully imported your books, they’ll show up in the middle pane and you can browse them from the left pane with filtering options such as authors, formats, ratings, news, tags and so forth.

When you right click on a selected ebook, it will provide a bunch of different option in one single pop-up from viewing,editing metadata to syncing with remote servers or by email. Converting ebooks is a breeze. You can choose whether you want to them to be converted individually or in bulk.

The news fetching feature is pretty decent too. You can schedule the news downloads from various different languages and you can add your own custom news sources at will.

Many thanks to Kovid Goyal, the creator of Calibre

All in all, Calibre is a mindblowing application which caters all your ebook managing needs without costing you a penny! Hope this post will enhance your productivity!
