How to Enable Timed Login in Ubuntu 10.04

Sep 19th, 2010 | Comments

Ubuntu gives you a plethora of inbuilt tools to take control of your PC while maintaining the simplicity. One such tool is the Timed Login. Most of you would don’t know what exactly is a Timed Login? The concept behind it is very simple. When you enable a timed login on guest account, you’ll still get a login prompt after reboot. If you don’t enter a username and password within X number of seconds, then the guest account will be used to login automatically.

Step 1 - Launching GDM Login Window

Navigate to System→Administration→Login Screen

Then Click on Unlock. After that you’ll be presented with the GDM Login Window. You will have to provide the password for authentication.

Step 2 - Configuring GDM Login Screen

Now click on the Login as radio button and select the guest account which you have created before. (If you need further explanation on creating a guest account then move on to the Alternate Step). Then enter the amount to be given for the user who’ll be logged before the guest account.

That’s all what you got to do!

Alternate Step

This step is intended to guide you on creating a new guest user account in Ubuntu.

Goto System→Administration→Users and Groups

Then click on Add and enter the Username and the shortname for it. In this case we will create it as guest.

After creating it, you can change the settings for that account by clicking on the Advanced Settings button. Then click on the User privileges tab. In here you can change the attributes according to your wish.

- Yeah! That’s all folks. Now head towards Step 2!

This function is mostly productive for situations like Internet Cafe’s and if don’t want your kids to mess with your PC with Admin rights! We would be glad to hear your comments!
