All Twitter Users Be-Aware! Your Account Might Be Hacked!

Mar 8th, 2010 | Comments

Recently, you might have seen lots of news about Twitter accounts being hacked! People will see unknown tweets sent through their own accounts! How can they hack our accounts like this? Lets find out…

First we got a phishing attack, which will tweet a link to a fake twitter login page and if the user enter their login details, his account will then be used to spread the spam message to all of his friends!

Next we are getting an attack which will tweet links to weight loss sites! How would you feel if your twitter account automatically tweets “Check out this diet I tried, it works!” followed by a URL to a weight loss site? I know.. one thing, you will become a big joke among your friends! Now, Sophos’s blog have a very good description of this attack : http://www.sophos.com/blogs/chetw/g/2010/03/06/john-dvorak-falls-victim-twitter-hacking/

How did the hackers launched these attacks?

There are two main methods :

  • Using the vulnerabilities of third-party twitter apps :

As in the video, first attack was launched by using a third-party twitter app! How many twitter apps do you see on the net? Do you see that much external apps for other social networks like Facebook? (im talking about external apps, not internal ones like “facebook applications”). And these third party apps allow us to do everything that we do on twitter! So, most of the people do not go to twitter to send their tweets.

Now, can we trust all these twitter apps? Actually, the first rule of the Internet is to trust no one! Even though the site is well trusted, can we trust the way in which they store our data? (is it secure enough? can anyone access our data?) Can we trust the connection that we make to their site, to give our details? Most of the time, the answer is “NO”!

  • Using Brute-Force attacks :

First of all, a Brute Force attack is used to crack your password by entering every possible combination into the password field. People suspect that the last attack was a Brute Force attack! The victims of this attack are the people who have weak passwords. A Brute Force attack is very successful, but its also very time consuming. Longer and more stronger passwords will take a long time to crack! (some times it might take years, so no point cracking them)

What should we do to protect against these attacks?

  1. Do not use any third party twitter app, unless you trust them!

  2. Use stronger passwords!

What to do if Im already hacked?

  • Inside Twitter, go to “Settings” > “Connections” (in the top right). Now, you’ll see all the third-party apps that you have granted access to your twitter account! Select the untrusted ones and click on “Revoke Access”

Note : In the above image I have chosen TweetMeme. But TweetMeme, is a completely safe application! Don’t worry, you can trust it without any problem!

  • Reset your password and give a new stronger one.

So, Are you a victim of these scams? What do you think about these scams? Please feel free to share your ideas with us!
