I Tried to Find an Alternative to Ubuntu... But...

May 14th, 2013 | Comments

Alternative to Ubuntu

I used Ubuntu as my primary OS for years. Within that period, they made several changes like making Unity the default which made me try other alternative to Ubuntu . But finally, I settled with Ubuntu again but replaced Unity with Gnome Shell. Later, after I had enough of Gnome Shell, I came back to Unity and started to like it. Now for me, Unity is the most suitable desktop! Many people move away from Ubuntu because of Unity. But I love Unity! Then what made me search for an alternative to Ubuntu?

Here’s why I wanted an alternative to Ubuntu :

- Its getting slow…

Ubuntu is getting slow. Mainly, its due to Unity. With the latest Ubuntu 13.04, they have made things fast. But they have much more to do. Normally, Linux is fast. Super fast… Windows and Mac doesn’t have a chance to beat Linux. But whats happening to Ubuntu? Is it becoming another Mac or another Windows?

- Less stable… More and more crash warnings…

Ubuntu 12.10 is one of the most unstable distributions of Ubuntu I have ever used. At first it threw crash warnings every hour. Later Canonical fixed some of these issues but it’s not enough. Ubuntu 13.04 is a bit better than its predecessor but it’s not enough!

-  Privacy Issues and lots of unwanted stuff…

With the introduction of online search, Ubuntu started to send our personal data to third-party companies. Of course we can disable it but by default its on. This raise a major question about privacy in Ubuntu? Is it safe to use the OS? The father of software and the man who created the GNU/Linux OS, Richard Stallman claimed that Ubuntu have malware!

So, I started to search for an alternative to Ubuntu…

- Debian

First, I tried Debian. For stability, its the best choice you got. I think its the most stable Linux distribution on earth. But to achive stability, you have to give up latest software updates. The Debian team do a lot of testing before releasing major versions. It might take nearly 1 to 3 years. So, they release with old software. After the release also hey don’t send major software updates. So, things get out of date very fast. Debian is extremely stable and secure… But its out of date!

- Arch and Manjaro Linux

Manjaro is based on Arch. Arch is the exact opposite of Debian. If a software release a new version, Arch might be the first Linux distribution to add the new version to their repositories. They have all the latest software. But because it doesn’t do much testing like Debian, things might break. I tried both Arch and Manjaro several times and even though I managed to install them, I face several issues after that. When we think about minimalism, Arch wins. Its one of the most minimal distributions on earth. You only have to install what you really need.

- Fedora and Open SUSE

Fedora didn’t simply had the features I wanted. Im already used to Debian based systems. But that doesn’t make its hard to adopt. Both Open SUSE and Fedora have heavy resource usage. That make them slow. They also doesn’t have much software like the Debian based systems. Because Ubuntu is the most popular distribution, many software were released specially for it.

Did I find an alternative to Ubuntu?

The simple answer is a big NO! It looks like Ubuntu is a bit unbeatable… I didn’t find a good alternative to Ubuntu !

It is not stable like Debian but I think its more stable than the other distributions I tried. Unity is a bit slow, but its not that bad and things are improving.

One major reason that I can’t find an alternative to Ubuntu is that it is unique. The look is unique, the software they offer, the features they offer… They are unique. For example, all the other distributions differ from the default system fonts used in Ubuntu. I’m not talking about the font family. I installed the Ubuntu font on other distributions but it didn’t look right. Later I learnt that Ubuntu is adding some special patches to its font rendering engine. The quality is great. They are smooth and very clear. None of the other distributions (except the ones based on Ubuntu) have such a quality. This is only one such example…

The software Ubuntu have by default, the changes they have made, the tweaks, the settings, they all make things easier for us. They are really unique. Apart from that, the support from the community is immense. You can find answers to any problem from the community. But remember, for Arch, you don’t need much help from the community because they have a very good documentation which describe every thing you need to know.

Now, I came back to my Ubuntu. No alternative to Ubuntu ! This is my personal taste. The distribution I picked that suit my needs. For you, it might change. But you’ll also face the same issue if you try to switch distributions. Have you every tried to do it? What happened?
