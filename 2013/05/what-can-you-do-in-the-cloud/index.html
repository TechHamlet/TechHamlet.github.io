What Can You Do in the Cloud?

May 30th, 2013 | Comments

The Cloud has come to revolutionise the way we not only interact with large amounts of data, but the way we store it as well. In recent years it has become a mainstay institution of many business sectors such as finance, accounting and human resources. But what can you actually do with it? Well, in short, a lot! Migrating a business’s data onto the Cloud is just the first step. Here are a few extremely important things which using the Cloud can allow a business to do.

Secure data storage

Of course, the most important and well-documented feature of the Cloud is its ability to store data online. What was once the chore of large location-based servers and hard drives carrying a company’s vital information such as client and customer details from a system like Sage One (which are commonly used in the accounting industry) is now stored online.

The huge virtual hard drives of the Cloud now allow this data to be stored online, meaning that it’s often a lot more secure than on a physical server which runs the risk of breaking down or crashing and losing a business’s valuable data forever.

Free up office space

Online data storage also has an extremely healthy benefit for office and technology business meaning the actual physical space taken up by physical hard drives and servers can be reduced, and more space created. There is also the fact that no depending on physical hardware may in the long run save a business a lot more money as they will not have to constantly invest in new machinery to accommodate ever-burgeoning amounts of data.

Remote access

The Cloud’s other chief point of interest is remote access. This means that when viewing, uploading or downloading any of a company’s data, you do not have to be in the exact location of the server. You can view this data from anywhere in the world – at home or on the move.

This is particularly useful in businesses which update their data frequently, such as accounting. Cloud accounting can allow a business to access, upload or alter any amount of valuable data wherever they are, at whatever time of the day they need to, and not have to wait until Monday morning.

Extra security

The security systems that are utilised by the cloud are much safer than the standard virus, malware and Trojan horse based anti-virus systems of most standard computers and databases. Storing valuable data online may seem like a bit of an oxymoron but it is generally much safer than on a smaller computer system, with a team’s software technicians constantly working to identify new threats.
