Popularity of Online Shopping Increased Through Social Media

May 30th, 2013 | Comments

Nowadays, more and more people are discovering the massive benefits of shopping online instead of the traditional way of going to a retail store in the neighborhood. In fact, it is possible to purchase almost any item online by simply looking for shops that offer the kind of products you have in mind.

Even at the comfort of your own home, you can shop for gadgets, clothes, food, and home appliances. If you want to save more money on an iPad, for instance, you can search for promos available online such as free delivery on iPads with this voucher. You will be amazed to discover a huge number of online stores that offer discounts and vouchers to customers.

Image Source : reway2007

What the Stats Say

Based on studies, younger consumers are more accustomed to shopping online, particularly those who are fond of using social media in searching for the right item to purchase. In fact, online shopping is already second nature to them, despite the less amount of quality time they get to spend with their shopping buddies at the mall.

While internet shopping is a popular option in several nations, researchers have discovered that most people from the UK are frequent online shoppers, as compared to any other countries in the world.

In a study by the Ofcom, UK consumers spend about £1,083 annually on items bought online, which place them at the top of the list. Moreover, these consumers are fond of shopping for mobile devices since they get to avail of discounts and special promos online.

Aside from online shopping, UK adults are engaged in social media networks such as Twitter and Facebook, and they use their mobile devices to access these sites.

This trend is even more evident in adults between 18 and 24 years old, as 62 percent of individuals in this age group are into mobile devices, online shopping and social media.

Why Shop Online

Primarily, people who prefer to buy items online are exploring the benefits of social shopping. Although they may lack face-to-face interactions with their friends each time they shop, they are still able to communicate with other shoppers by visiting social sites.

For instance, there are brand sites that enable them to create a community or participate in forums and talk about their opinions of a product bought online. They may also spread the word about a recent promo they have seen in sites such as a 15% off all credit packs at iStockPhoto or massive discounts for electronics and devices.

These sites serve as a place to bring all shoppers together and narrate their online shopping-related experiences, which can guide other consumers, as well.

You can also come across social media websites that revolve around product ratings, reviews, suggestions and opinions. It is also quite easy to make your own shopping lists online, join groups and ask for product suggestions from your contacts. Then, you can boost your savings by checking out location-based sites for the latest promos or discounts available.

Online shopping has reinvented the way people buy things, and social media has made the entire process of internet buying even more fun and more convenient than ever. It is now hassle-free to check reviews and forums about a product to maximize your chances of finding the perfect item online that is worth your time and money.
