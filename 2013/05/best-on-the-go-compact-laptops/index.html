Best On-the-go Compact Laptops for You!

May 17th, 2013 | Comments

Image source : Michael Sheehan

When laptops first appeared on the market, choices were limited, making shopping for one a fairly simple process. However, whether for personal or professional use, finding the best compact laptop that comes with all the tools needed at a low price is a daunting task. One method of purchasing a new compact laptop while reducing expenses is to sell your netbook and other old electrical devices in order to have more liquidity to use toward the new laptop.

Preserving the battery life

Recent technological advances such as improved low-power LED screens place lesser demands on battery life. Laptops are more durable with materials used by the defense industry carried across into civilian merchandise. The international research firm IDG released a report stating that notebook sales controlled an approximate 70 per cent share of the PC market in 2012.  This suggests that increasing numbers of the business labour force are using portable computers. After much research, four laptops stood out from the pack as great buys.

First on the list is the 15-inch, MacBook Pro 2.4GHz. Although it may seem quite expensive at first, this little number has virtually everything needed. Along with the Intel Core i5 processor, they include 4GB of RAM, providing enough horsepower for multiple programs, except heavy graphics. A little more cash will send you on your way with Microsoft Office Mac Home and Student Edition, which is perfect for those on the go. However, it has a small screen and a limited keyboard. No optical drives are included, so factor in additional peripherals.

The second favourite was Hewlett-Packard’s Mini 311. This nearly full-size device has an 11.6-inch screen, HDMI output and Windows 7 OS. This trooper is the business worker’s favoured netbook option. Mind you, netbook models do not have CD or media drives. They are ideal for basic cloud-based software and apps. If your computing needs are minimal, this is just the model for you.

The harder-edged option

Some individuals have a tendency to be a little rougher with their possessions. For these people, their laptop might look like it was in Kandahar recently. If this sounds familiar, then look to the rugged laptop by Getac P470. There is no real ‘wow’ factor, but the 14-inch screen is great for outdoor use and it has a respectably-sized keyboard. The owner can access it using a smart card reader, a fingerprint reader or the Trusted Platform Chip. These will guard your content from inquisitive eyes.

Dell’s Vostro V13 is elegant and sleek. From Dell’s harrowing market tumble emerged a brilliant champion. The Vostro V13 comes with 2GB of RAM and a 13-inch screen. There are metal hinges and a smart arrangement of media connectors. These coupled with Vostro’s colour make it simply beautiful. These are very easy on the wallet but offer all the power of other portable PCs.
