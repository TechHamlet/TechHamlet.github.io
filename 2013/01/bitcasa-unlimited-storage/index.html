Bitcasa Offers Unlimited Cloud Storage! You'll Never Run Out of Space!

Jan 2nd, 2013 | Comments

Bitcasa

Normally, we have limits in everything. Our hard drives have limited capacity, portable drives (USB drives, Portable Hard Drives) have limited capacity, Cloud storage services like Dropbox, Google Drive, SugarSync and Box have limited capacity. If we hit the limit on our hard drives, we have to spend more money to upgrade. If we hit the limit in a cloud storage service, we have to spend more money to go to a better plan. But unlike hard drives, these services have a very small capacity. Both of them have limits. But… Bitcasa doesn’t have a limit!

As I said earlier, online cloud storage services have a very limited capacity. Even if you start to pay, you will always hit a limit. But Bitcasa say they can offer us unlimited storage! You might think… If its infinite storage, then it will cost a lot! No… There are two plans… The first is a free plan where you’ll get 10 GB of free space. 10 GB! Dropbox offer 2 GB and Google Drive offer 5 GB of free space. Still its big. But if you need truly infinite storage, you can upgrade to the next plan which is only $10 per month! Cool isn’t it? For that price, Dropbox will give you 500 GB of free space and Google Drive will give 200 GB. Bitcasa… No limit!

According to Bitcasa, they were able to offer unlimited storage because of a brand new technology they use. I tried to get more details on what enabled them to give unlimited storage but couldn’t find any good explanations. So, it’s a big mystery.

Like most of the other cloud storage services, you have to download and install the Bitcasa client on your machine. After installation, Bitcasa will create a separate drive on your computer. Anything you send to this drive will be synced with their service. In services like Dropbox, what we put into the folder will remain in our machine even after syncing. But Bitcasa files won’t be stored in our machine. There’s a good reason… If they store the files locally, then they can’t provide unlimited storage. It will be limited to the capacity of our local hard drive. But there’s a down side of this method… If you have a slow Internet connection, accessing these files will be painful.

I tried Bitcasa and uploaded my first file which weighed nearly 1.2 Gigs. First, Bitcasa stores the file in a local cache and then starts uploading. Even if you reboot your machine, it will resume the transfer without starting all over again. But, in a slow connection, it can take most of your bandwidth during transfers. In Dropbox, you won’t notice much difference because they reduce their speed to allow us to do our day-to-day work. So, Bitcasa need to improve on that side…

After uploading your files, you can access them from any machine you like and can even share them with your friends. Currently they have native apps for Linux, Mac, Windows (Also have a separate Windows 8 app),  Android, Chrome and the Web Access. The iPhone app is on the way. But the Linux support is not enough. It only supports Ubuntu and won’t even work in Linux Mint. But I think they’ll fix it soon…

Bitcasa is new and they are still growing. They have more to improve. But… They are unique and I don’t think there’s any other service which offer you unlimited storage! So, what will you store in your new drive?
