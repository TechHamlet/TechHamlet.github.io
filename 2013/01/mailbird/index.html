Mailbird : A More Elegant, Fast and a Better Mail Client for Windows

Jan 8th, 2013 | Comments

Mailbird

Everyday we use emails. We send emails, we read emails and we get mad by just dealing with emails. I don’t know about you but its a real pain for me to deal with these EMAILS! Isn’t there a better way to deal with them? A better mail client?

Actually, we do have a lot of different clients to deal with email. First, we have their own native web clients. Gmail, Yahoo, Microsoft… all have their own UIs. But some people need a better way to read emails rather than opening another browser tab, entering the password every time and face more troubles… We need something that can sit on our desktop and something that we can easily access anytime we want. Do we have such desktop clients? Yes we do!

The most popular email client is Thunderbird. I used it for several months and gave it up and went back to web mail! Why? The software is very heavy on resources and it almost crash my system. Its even very slow to load emails. Takes a lot of time to sync and I got enough of their old UI. Then, they announced that they won’t add much changes for Thunderbird! What else do we have? We have more email clients but are they better than Thunderbird?

I think we have something better…

Recently, Tom Lunche (one of our loyal readers) wrote an email to us mentioning about a better mail client named Mailbird. I immediately checked it and signed in for the beta. Few days ago, I received my first beta invitation from Andrea. So, I started testing Mailbird!

My first impressions on Mailbird?

With the first look, I can say that Mailbird is the best looking Windows mail client. It’s not cluttered… There are no bloat… Everything is simple. To make things even more simple, Mailbird will take you through an initial introduction to explain their key features.

Everything is simple and easy to access…

You have your folders on the left bar followed by another bar containing the messages. The message previews are next. Like in Gmail, the previews are in a threaded format so you can easily identify and refer to previous emails. It’s not in one big mess like many other clients. In Gmail, the latest conversation will be displayed at the bottom. So, we need to keep scrolling to get there. But in Mailbird, it’s on the top. Easy access!

In their initial tutorial, Mailbird will teach you some shortcuts which can be used with the program. C for compose, R for reply, ! to mark spam, F4 to find a message… Most of the shortcuts have one key which is easy to remember.

Options

Easy integration with Gmail… No advanced setup needed…

Sometimes, you need to specially set some advanced settings in-order to link your Gmail account with a desktop email client. But recently, most of them started to add easy quick integration. Mailbird also have this feature. You just have to add the email and the password. The app will take care of the advanced settings.

In Gmail, Google have added a special feature called important emails. It will mark the most important emails according to what we read and with who we communicate. Desktop mail clients doesn’t show these marks. But in Mailbird, you can activate them!

Not only Gmail, the developers have promised to add other systems like Hotmail in upcoming releases. The ability to add multiple accounts will also be released soon.

Connect your Facebook account and see the faces of your contacts…

If you connect your Facebook account, Mailbird will match your contacts with their Facebook accounts by checking the emails address and add their profile picture to the contact list. Now you can see who you are talking to!

Its Fast! Really fast…

If you take a mail client like Thunderbird, its UI is really slow… Takes a long time to open a message. But Mailbird is fast. It’s really fast. The loading of emails, replying and composing all are fast. But it comes with a small price. The app take some resources to make things fast. The memory and the CPU usage is a bit high. Not too much but it can be a problem for notebook under battery power. But the app won’t slow down the system… The developers have released an update to fix some of the CPU issues today and Im sure they’ll fix everything soon. It will be much better if they can let the app switch to a low power mode when the machine is on battery power!

Finally the Apps!

The Mailbird system is like a whole new platform! You can build open source apps for the system and help it to extend its ability. Currently, there are some default apps like Facebook, Dropbox, Contacts, Asna Productivity App, Google Drive, Daily Social, and some more… It looks simple but it’s really powerful…

The Apps

First of all, ask yourself… Do you need a mail client that’s packed with loads of features where you’ll use only about 10% of them? Do you need a mail client that’s really slow? Or, do you want one simple app that can do its simple job perfectly? I think that’s what we want right now… Mailbird is easy to explore and there are no much complex features. But I just thought… Do we need so many features? Its simple and does its job perfectly. What else do we need? I think Mailbird have everything we need. Don’t you think you should give it a try? Unfortunately, it’s currently under Beta and only people with an invitation can try it. But the Pro version will be available. So, if you have the guts, try it!

Before wrapping up… I need to say that I’m waiting for the developers to release a Linux version soon! I hope they’ll consider it :)
