Why You Should Use Email Marketing in 2013

Jan 17th, 2013 | Comments

Okay so the Mayans might not have actually accurately predicted the end of the world in December 2012. As a result the year as a whole changed from being “pretty climatic” to “pretty uneventful”. But whether you were eagerly anticipating a real-life disaster movie or not, 2012 was not a complete waste of time. Technology is constantly evolving at a faster and faster pace, and 2012 was no different. 3D printers advanced significantly, and created whole house in one session; people can now control robot limbs with their minds. But besides those amazing technological game-changers for the whole of humanity, there were also some more specific advances. Email marketing, for instance, has come a long way since its initial conception. This is why 2013 is an exciting year – it’s a year when all of these technological advancements can be fully utilized  and it’s as good a time as ever to jump on to the things that could be beneficial to you as a business. You should definitely be considering using email marketing in 2013 if you’re not using it already.

One of the things that is great about email marketing in comparison to other more traditional forms of marketing is that it’s comparatively quite cheap and cost-effective. The major costs are towards things like web hosting and a domain licence, neither of which cost very much. It will also cost a little bit for membership for a good email marketing service that will help with your campaign’s organisation. Compare to the costs of something like a film advertising campaign, the costs are significantly cheaper.

Email marketing is a great way to actually reach people in a non-passive way, too. As the years slog on, more and more people are getting online. It’s an oddity for someone to not be a member of Facebook, let alone not have access to any kind of email service. And email services are something that are actually looked over and checked attentively by most people. When you drop in that perfectly crafter email you’re dropping it into quite a personal space. This has both it’s advantage and disadvantages – but all of them can work very much in your favor if you put in the work. A nicely crafted, personalized email campaign towards the right demographic can be far from being nuisance spam mail – but instead an asset to the receiver, allowing you to build up a rapport and a relationship with the people you’re marketing towards on a level unattainable by more traditional shotgun approaches like a really big billboard on eighth street.

Email marketing is the height of modernity when it comes to the marketing world. It is a marketing tool that really takes advantage of 21st century first-world societal norms. By making sure you approach email subscriber lists in the right way you can really create a great, down-to-earth relationship on a personal level with your subscribers and avoid that nasty, grimy “marketing” feeling. The internet is all about connectivity and email marketing is really something that takes advantage of that to make sure that you are connected to the right people when marketing your business. Email marketing is a great way to forge new relationships with the people you want to be taking notice of your business, and it really is something you should be checking out this year, because it’s a big thing now, and it’s only going to get bigger. Make sure you get in early to get the best possible experience you can.
