Rural Broadband Upgrade Approved

Jan 11th, 2013 | Comments

Broadband

Almost a year ago plans for a £530 million nationwide broadband upgrade for the most rural areas in England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland were submitted by the government, though it has taken this long to gain approval from the European Commission. With little word from the EU since January when the plans were first formulated, the Culture Secretary Maria Miller held talks in Brussels towards the end of November, with a view to gaining approval for the broadband roll out.

Thankfully for Brits who live in more remote regions in the UK, approval has been granted, and the government can press on with its vision. The EU Commission said that everything involved was above board, and that it was likely that the plans would have been approved anyway; though Mrs Miller has almost certainly sped up the process, at least by a few weeks.

The government’s aim has been to deliver “the best broadband network in Europe,” according to Mrs Miller, by expanding and improving the broadband options in regions where internet from commercial retailers is not easily accessible, with parts of Wales looking set to be among the first areas to benefit.

A spokesperson for Make It Cheaper, one of the UK’s leading comparison sites, said: “Since online purchasing in the UK will increase 11% per year for the next four years, government initiatives to bridge the existing digital divide arrived at an opportune time. The £530m investment to upgrade Britain’s broadband infrastructure will make the UK economy more competitive in global e-commerce and it will allow SMEs in rural areas to advertise and sell their products on a new platform and to expand their customer base.”

More of the UK’s business than ever before is conducted online; and that does not just mean B2B, but B2C too. The spokesperson for energy comparison experts Make It Cheaper added: “Furthermore, with improved connectivity, businesses will be able to access online services faster, which will enable them to communicate with their own suppliers and government organisations in a more efficient manner. While the majority of the new rural broadband scheme will be carried out by BT, local businesses are advised to check if there are other suppliers that offer broadband services for a better price in their region.”
