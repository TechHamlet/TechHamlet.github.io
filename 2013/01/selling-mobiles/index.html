Selling Mobiles : eBay vs Gumtree vs Phone Recycling?

Jan 10th, 2013 | Comments

No one would argue that mobile devices have become an integral part of our everyday life. Mobile technology companies always have something to offer in an effort to satiate our endless need and enthusiasm for mobile gadgets. Smartphone operating systems are constantly upgraded. It is projected that by mid 2013, more than half all the mobile phone handsets will function on operating systems designed for smartphones. As more and more mobile devices are produced, do we even wonder what happens to old and obsolete handsets? And how do you exactly make profit out of a used handset? There are three ways to do so.

Selling it through local classifieds or free advertisement sites

Local newspapers and circulated publications would always have a section dedicated to classified advertisements. It is also possible to advertise on the Internet. Sellers only need to create an account on advertisement sites, post pictures of their device for sale with its specifications, and wait for prospect buyers to contact them.

Advertising online is particularly advantageous because it costs less than newspaper advertisements. The disadvantage in this method of selling is that the seller does not meet the buyer in person. Furthermore, buyers may bargain and ask for a lower price.

Selling it through mobile phone recycling companies

Of all the selling methods, it is recycling that probably posts the least number of risks and issues. In this method, a mobile phone recycling company offers sellers a quote based on the condition of their device. The device is forwarded to the company, where they examine its existing condition. Devices of good quality are reconditioned so they could be sold in other countries. Devices with malfunctions beyond repair are simply stripped of their usable components.

Each company would quote a different price, depending on the quality and state of the device. Sellers are advised to turn to comparison sites like CompareMyMobile.com to have an idea on how much does a particular mobile handset value. Comparison sites usually have the best deals for secondhand mobile devices.

Selling through auction sites

Auction sites are a favorite place for finding deals on anything and everything. Millions of buyers and sellers transact on auction sites like eBay on a daily basis. However, many consumers still feel apprehensive about advertising on auction sites. First, auction sites do not always guarantee a sale. Furthermore, there is always a risk of falling victim to unscrupulous individuals who use auction sites to scam others.

The best thing about auction sites, though, is they allow sellers to create a full auction list that includes the device’s pictures and detailed specifications. However, auction sites like eBay has initial listing fee and a 10% stake of the final sales price of the item.

Where would the best place to sell be?!

According to the data CompareMyMobile.com has collected over time, it shows that if you are planning to sell an old model of a smartphone, you are likely to recieve a similar price through a mobile phone recycler than you would have done through an auction site like eBay.

Smartphones that have been released within 12 months would generally receive more by selling through the classified ads. The return is roughly about 20% more than that of a smartphone recycler. However, if selling directly to the public face to face you need to take into account that they may barter the price thus making the sale the same as what a recycler may have paid.

By looking at the figures, it shows that eBay, on average, sell 10% more than a mobile phone recycler would pay. The problem selling with eBay would be that there is a lot of competition for the same item, so your listing would have to stand out from the crowd and entice buyers to place a bid on your item. If your listing does not recieve many views you may have to accept that a lower price may be paid for your item, along with ebay and paypal fees could return in a lesser amount than the recyclers would have paid.

To conclude, your best bet would be to sell to a trusted mobile phone recycler as this is the safest and more sensible option. Simply search for compare mobile phone recycling, choose your handset that you wish to sell, from the list of recyclers choose the one that offers the best price and await for them to send their postage bag.
