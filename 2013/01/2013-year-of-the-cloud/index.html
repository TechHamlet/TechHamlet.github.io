2013: Year of the Cloud?

Jan 9th, 2013 | Comments

The Mayans may not have accurately predicted the end of the world in 2012, but that doesn’t mean there weren’t any big worldwide changes at all. They just didn’t involve fire, meteorites, or death. And as far as most people are concerned that’s a good thing. 2012 was a great year for technological advancements, though. People can now control robotic limbs with their minds, 3D printers can now build houses in one session, and cloud technology became more prolific and recognised than ever before. As far as cloud technology is concerned, things are only looking up for the future. 2012 was good, but can 2013 really be the year of the cloud? If you have adopted it yet, then maybe this will be the year you should definitely make the plunge.

Cloud storage is pretty simply just the virtualisation of data. Data is not stored on-site in physical servers, but instead offsite by a dedicated company. That cloud storage company has many of their own servers, and the section you pay them to use are all virtually collected together into one “cloud” space that you and your business can access anywhere, anytime as long as you have an internet connection to get to it. You can also have multiple people accessing the same data at once. To put it in even more basic terms cloud storage is essentially turning your data into magic dust that can be drawn from the sky above as long as you have an internet connection. Let’s be honest, the internet is basically magic anyway though, right?

In recent years there has been an increasing demand on technology dealing with data. And it only looks set to continue that trend through 2013. There constantly needs to be more and more data space available to more and more people. There also needs to be more instant and on-demand access to data. Trend Micro cloud storage is definitely a response to a lot of growing needs – an answer to many problems facing a lot of businesses that have to handle a lot of data, which is most businesses nowadays. It can also relieve a lot of pressure for small-middling enterprises as no internal staff has to be dedicated to the managing and maintenance of physical hardware for data, allowing the core of the business to be focused on substantially internally. Off-loading data maintenance to a third party can really free you up to focus on perhaps more important things.

Cloud storage offers a tonne – or to use a more apt measurement system, terabytes – of benefits and advantages for a lot of people in a time of increasing technological importance. It is especially useful for business that handle data. The necessity of cloud storage is only something that is going to increase over time, so there’s really no reason to not be jumping onto the bandwagon and reaping the benefits immediately. 2013 is definitely the year you want to be doing so if you really haven’t considered it yet. Make 2013 a good one and at least check it out.
