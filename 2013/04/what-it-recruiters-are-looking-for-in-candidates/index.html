What IT Recruiters Are Looking for in Candidates

Apr 8th, 2013 | Comments

When job hunting, the first thing candidates tend to think about is what they are looking for in a job. Salary expectations, working hours and workplace environment are all important considerations to make when applying for job vacancies but it’s not just job-seekers who are looking for certain qualities. Employers have their own check-list to work from and knowing what is listed on here for your industry is the key to achieving recruitment success.

#1. IT industry

Whilst IT is now a central part of almost every workplace, those seeking specific IT jobs in London or anywhere else in the country will be even more connected with the world of computers. The prevalence of these modern machines means that competition is fierce and one advertised vacancy could attract plenty of interest. Those interested in applying must therefore demonstrate the highest level of aptitude and a comprehensive skill-set to stand any chance of success – but exactly which skills are in the highest demand?

#2. Social media

It may seem a strange starting point but social media really is a vital tool for any job hunter – whether applying within the IT industry or not. At the end of 2011, research revealed that as much as 91% screened job applicants using social media. Of those viewing the profiles of their prospective employees, just under half of them did so immediately after receiving the application.

For those hoping to work in the IT industry, knowledge and appropriate use of these systems is therefore a must. Not only do you need to show your ability to adapt to emerging technologies but you also need to make sure your profile paints you in the best possible light. After all, corporate responsibility and safe surfing are mainstays of the modern computing world and any IT recruiter will be looking for a candidate who not only understands computing machines but also has knowledge of how they impact other areas of our life.

#3. Versatility

Social media is just one avenue explored by the IT industry though and successful candidates are those who demonstrate versatility and adaptability. You need to be on top of your game when it comes to applying for IT jobs but this doesn’t just mean focusing on what is new. Going back to basics and refreshing your memory over basic systems such as the Microsoft Office Suite is always recommended – after all, these are the systems which are used throughout numerous business, industries and environments.

Knowledge of the different packages, how they work and how to use them more efficiently is a real asset in the fast-paced world of technology and it would be a shame to miss out on a job opportunity purely because you overlooked these core skills.

#4. Personality

Lastly, it is not just technical skills which employers will be looking for – they’ll want to see your personality too. Ultimately, recruiters are looking for candidates who can fit seamlessly into existing workplaces and work productively from the outset. Personal skills are an asset in every industry but IT recruitment agency Randstad Technologies can help those looking for IT jobs identify the skills they need and how to go about obtaining them.
