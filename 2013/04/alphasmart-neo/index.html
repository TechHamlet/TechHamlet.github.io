What Is Alphasmart Neo and How It Can Be Used in the Classroom

Apr 27th, 2013 | Comments

Image Source : Robert Burdock

The Alphasmart Neo is a relatively inexpensive and durable word processor that has been especially designed for use by children and young adults but it has also proved wildly popular with all sorts of people involved with writing because of its unique features.

Its portability and impressive AA battery life of 900 hours, tough design and immediate start-up make it ideal for writing in any environment and the fact that there are no distractions such as internet connectivity and a plethora of programmes means that the user can concentrate exclusively on writing technique.

For kids especially, the Neo can be something of a culture shock at first but they quickly come to love it and there’s plenty of support from teachers for its increasingly widespread use in the classroom.

Special needs students who are hampered by dyslexia or lack of fine motor skills or who have limited verbal communication abilities have been shown to benefit greatly from using the Neo which has assistive technology built in and available add-ons that can effectively tailor it to the needs of both children and adults who have learning and physical challenges.

The full-sized keyboard has ‘sticky’ keys, so that combination keystrokes can be typed without the need to press different keys at the same time. Children who have difficulty in targeting the keys or who have unsteady hands also benefit from the slow keys feature and problems with reflex control are assisted by the auto-repeat function which can be turned off and on at the touch of a button.

The small screen on which the words and sentences appear can be adjusted to display several grades of size, from very small with six lines to extra large font options which are ideal for anyone with visual challenges. There are also various so-called SmartApplets available, software which extends the Neo’s capabilities by allowing for such things as word-prediction to assist with composition and the creation of sentences that are grammatically correct. Skills in grammar, fluency and expression can all be improved with the text-to-speech option, which converts the words that have been written into spoken words.

There are numerous endorsements from parents of children with conditions such as Asperger’s Syndrome and dyslexia who have been delighted when their child who has always struggled with handwriting starts using the Neo and takes to it like a fish to water, with excitement and confidence in writing typically increasing from the outset.

Enhancing their learning

In addition to devices such as these, picking up an external hard drive or other storage device can also help children protect their work and keep everything safe. Whether it’s a solid state drive or even a portable hard drive which you opt for, these units are a great option for those looking to help those with learning difficulties adapt to modern technology. When used in conjunction with devices such as the Alphasmart Neo you’re sure to notice an improvement, with kids embracing new gadgets and learning the importance of keeping their hard work safe and well protected.
